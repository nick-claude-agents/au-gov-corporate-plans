IP Australia — Business Development Brief

Generated 31 May 2026 · Source corporate plan
IP Australia
Industry, Science and Resources · Corporate Plan 2024-25 · BD confidence: Medium

IP Australia is a fully cost-recovered regulator and administrator of Australia's IP rights system (patents, trade marks, designs, plant breeder's rights) and the Trans-Tasman attorney regime. The 2024-25 plan signals a deliberate sharpening around financial sustainability/efficiency, contemporary digital and data-driven customer services, AI's disruptive impact on examination, and workforce capability under the APS Reform agenda. As a cost-recovered agency exposed to volatile filing patterns, it runs a rolling ten-year investment plan and an ICT Strategy 2022-2025 that is due for refresh. A live ANAO performance audit into AI use in patent examination and an ANAO cost-recovery audit are the strongest near-term external demand signals. Best-fit opportunities lie in Audit & Assurance (AI/ADM governance), Financial & Accounting (cost-recovery/pricing), Digital & ICT Delivery, and Data, Analytics & Information — proportionate to a mid-sized regulator with own-source revenue.

Strategic priorities

  • Financial sustainability and efficiency of a cost-recovered agency — The DG added 'efficiency' to Strategic Objective 1.1 and emphasises cost-effective service delivery, fee consistency under the Charging Framework, and managing exposure to filing-pattern volatility. Reducing IT cost and outsourcing (~$950k saving target in 2024-25) is explicit.
  • Contemporary digital and data-driven customer/staff services — Strategic Objective 4.2 commits to innovative digital solutions, emerging technologies that automate services, and data-driven decision-making, governed by the ICT Strategy 2022-2025 (refreshed yearly) and the Data, Information and Analytics Strategy 2023-2026.
  • Responding to generative AI's impact on the IP system — The plan states IP Australia is examining how generative AI is disrupting the IP rights system and how it should respond to remain fit-for-purpose while balancing rights owners and AI innovators — now the subject of a live ANAO audit of AI in examination.
  • Workforce capability uplift under APS Reform — People Strategy 2024-2027 focuses on flexibility, innovation, capability uplift and culture, alongside reducing outsourcing in core functions under the APS Strategic Commissioning Framework.
  • Reforms to the regulated attorney profession and IP legislation — Significant initiatives include delivering agreed reforms to the regulated attorney profession on behalf of the Australian and New Zealand governments, and evidence-based legislative improvements including Indigenous Knowledge protection.
  • Indigenous Knowledge protection and awareness — Building on the May 2024 WIPO Diplomatic Conference, IP Australia commits to further reforms to protect First Nations traditional knowledge and cultural expressions, domestically and internationally.

Reform & change drivers

  • Generative AI disrupting patent/trade mark examination and the broader IP rights system, now under live ANAO scrutiny
  • APS Reform agenda and APS Strategic Commissioning Framework driving insourcing of core roles (~$950k outsourcing reduction in 2024-25)
  • Cost-recovery model under the Australian Government Charging Framework exposing the agency to filing-volatility-driven financial sustainability risk
  • ICT Strategy 2022-2025 nearing end of cycle, requiring refresh; rolling ten-year capital investment plan
  • Data, Information and Analytics Strategy 2023-2026 maturing data governance and analytics capability
  • Agreed reforms to the Trans-Tasman regulated attorney profession
  • International IP reform momentum (WIPO traditional knowledge/genetic resources outcomes) requiring legislative and policy follow-through
  • New consolidated enterprise strategy and performance framework following a comprehensive review

Challenges & risks

  • Financial sustainability tied to economic conditions and unpredictable IP filing patterns for a cost-recovered agency
  • Minimising overall IT and maintenance cost while continuing to modernise systems
  • Integrating AI/automated decision-making into examination without undermining quality, trust and accountability
  • Workforce capability and integrity risks while reducing reliance on highly specialised contractors
  • Maintaining high timeliness, quality (>85%) and online availability (>98%) commitments under cost pressure
  • Cyber/information and data security of customer-facing rights systems
  • Governance and assurance load across multiple committees (Audit, IIPC, Information and Data, People, Strategic Policy)
