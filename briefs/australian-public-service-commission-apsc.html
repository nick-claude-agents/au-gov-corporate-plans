Australian Public Service Commission (APSC)
Prime Minister and Cabinet · Corporate Plan 2025-29 (budget year 2025-26) · BD confidence: Medium

The APSC is a small, expertise-heavy central agency (policy/regulatory archetype) acting as steward of the APS on integrity, workforce management and capability. Its 2025-29 plan is organised around three pillars - integrity, capability and workforce management - and reveals active reform agendas: the Strategic Commissioning Framework (insourcing core work), the Integrity Roadmap (2023-26), an APS-wide reform program (including Closing the Gap and diversity Outcome 6), and the APS Academy's capability uplift. Internally, it is mid-stream on a Capability Review action plan (73.7% complete), replacing its ERP and case management systems, rolling out modern workplace tools, standing up AI governance, and developing a workforce plan and National Property Strategy. An ANAO performance audit has found the APSC lacks a documented strategy and effectiveness measures for its own stewardship/integrity functions - a direct, citable advisory signal. Because the APSC itself owns the insourcing agenda, any pursuit must be framed around capability transfer and short, defensible advisory rather than long-term outsourced delivery.

Strategic priorities

  • Strengthening APS integrity and pro-integrity culture — Delivering the Integrity Roadmap (2023-26), embedding integrity initiatives as business-as-usual, running an Integrity Communications and Engagement Strategy, and evaluating how agencies uphold the APS Values and Code of Conduct.
  • Implementing the Strategic Commissioning Framework (insourcing core work) — Driving the APS-wide shift to bring core work in-house, with 2025-26 targets focused on reduced outsourcing of service delivery and >90% of agencies meeting reporting milestones.
  • Building APS capability and leadership via the APS Academy — Delivering leadership and talent programs and APS Craft learning, with new effectiveness measures, and implementing the APS Learning and Development Strategy Adapt Action Plan.
  • Workforce management policy and diversity — Shaping remuneration and conditions, ensuring collective agreement compliance, lifting First Nations SES representation (currently 2.86%) and meeting APS Reform Outcome 6 (equity, inclusion, diversity) metrics.
  • Internal technology and corporate modernisation — Implementing a new case management system, replacing the ERP, rolling out modern workplace tools, establishing AI governance, and uplifting cyber, information and data management under the 2024-27 technology strategy.
  • Completing Capability Review uplift and embedding evaluation — Finishing the remaining Capability Review action-plan initiatives and embedding new governance and evaluation mechanisms to monitor performance against outcomes.

Reform & change drivers

  • Strategic Commissioning Framework requiring APS-wide reduction in outsourcing and insourcing of core work (initial period 2024-25 to 2026-27)
  • Integrity Roadmap fixed-term (2023-26) and broader integrity reform following the Robodebt Royal Commission
  • APS Reform agenda including Outcome 6 (equity, inclusion, diversity) and Closing the Gap Priority Reform Three
  • Public Service Act 1999 statutory functions and Public Sector Workplace Relations Policy compliance obligations
  • Internal Capability Review (July 2023) 12-action response plan over a 5-year horizon
  • Technology strategy 2024-27 - ERP replacement, new case management system, AI governance, cyber and data uplift
  • Financial uncertainty named as a strategic risk constraining capacity to meet growing stakeholder demand
  • Competitive labour market and skills shortages affecting both the Commission and the wider APS

Challenges & risks

  • Inability to sustain capacity and capabilities to meet growing delivery demands 'in an environment of significant financial uncertainty' (named strategic risk)
  • Risk of not meeting legislative obligations or failing to steward APS institutional integrity
  • Risk of losing stakeholder confidence as a valued, credible and trusted partner
  • Difficulty aligning workplace conditions with labour-market expectations to attract and retain talent
  • Stagnant First Nations SES representation (2.86%) against Closing the Gap commitments
  • New and still-maturing performance measures - several with baselines only and targets 'to be determined'
  • ANAO finding that the APSC lacks a documented strategy and effectiveness measures for its stewardship/integrity functions
  • Self-imposed constraint: as owner of the insourcing agenda, the APSC must limit reliance on external providers

ANAO audit findings

  • ANAO performance audit 'Australian Public Service Commission's Administration of Integrity Functions' found the APSC did not have a documented strategy or plan to support the Commissioner's stewardship/partnering function, could not measure its effectiveness in that function, and was 'largely activity-driven' rather than strategically planned. (ANAO - https://www.anao.gov.au/work/performance-audit/australian-public-service-commissions-administration-integrity-functions)
    Direct, named advisory opportunity: strategy design, performance-measurement frameworks and stewardship-function planning are gaps the auditor itself identified - proposals can be anchored to specific ANAO findings.
