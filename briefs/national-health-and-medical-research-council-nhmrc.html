National Health and Medical Research Council (NHMRC)
Health (Health, Disability and Ageing) · Corporate Plan 2025-26 (covering 2025-26 to 2028-29) · BD confidence: Medium

NHMRC is an independent statutory authority and Australia's primary health-and-medical-research funder, operating as a hybrid research-funder/regulator/guidelines body. This year its deliberate agenda is dominated by enabling and reform work: supporting development and 2026 implementation of Australia's first National Health and Medical Research Strategy, harmonising the MRFF and MREA grant programs under shared advisory structures, refreshing the Sapphire grants management platform, stabilising financial sustainability, reforming peer review, and strengthening research integrity. External pressure is real and current — record-low Ideas Grant success rates (~8%) and sector criticism of peer-review fairness make process redesign, evaluation and assurance politically live. The cleanest BD fits are Digital & ICT Delivery (Sapphire/ICT/data), Financial & Accounting (financial-sustainability uplift), Strategy, Policy & Governance (peer-review/evaluation/strategy), and Audit & Assurance (grants integrity), though NHMRC's APS Strategic Commissioning Framework target to insource core ICT roles tempers some technology demand.

Strategic priorities

  • Support the National Health and Medical Research Strategy — Work with the Department of Health, Disability and Ageing to finalise and release Australia's first National Health and Medical Research Strategy, with public consultation across H2 2025 and implementation from 2026.
  • Harmonise the MRFF and MREA — Optimise synergies and align policy, operations and governance between the MRFF (administered with the Department) and NHMRC's MREA grant program, using the shared NHMRC-MRFF advisory committee structure in place since 1 July 2024.
  • Maintain excellence in peer review — Innovate, evaluate and enhance how researchers are assessed for grants — peer reviewer training, expanding consumer roles, refining score descriptors, feedback to applicants and reducing reviewer burden.
  • Bolster confidence in research integrity — With the ARC, progress potential reform of the national research integrity framework and update NHMRC policies for managing misconduct, fraud and corruption, including releasing the Good Institutional Practice Guide.
  • Refresh the Sapphire grants platform — Embark on a refresh of NHMRC's end-to-end grant management system, shifting how Sapphire is hosted, supported and developed to build a centralised, maintainable, resilient platform that reduces administrative burden and optimises data capability.
  • Stabilise financial sustainability — Mature oversight and assurance over the annual operating budget and project investment by embedding a robust multi-year internal budget and stronger financial governance and compliance frameworks.
  • Optimise ICT and data capability — Mature core ICT and data capabilities through service-delivery, ICT governance, project delivery and data/information management practices, including launching an ICT Strategy and finalising a Data and Information Strategy.
  • Foster translation and commercialisation — Refresh NHMRC's Research Translation Strategy for 2026 and beyond and expand industry engagement (biotech and health-connected companies) to drive commercialisation and local production of treatments and devices.

Reform & change drivers

  • First-ever National Health and Medical Research Strategy being finalised in 2025 for implementation from 2026, setting a unified national direction and reshaping coordination of the research ecosystem
  • Harmonisation of MRFF and MREA grant programs under shared NHMRC-MRFF advisory committees (in effect since 1 July 2024)
  • Sapphire grants platform refresh — a shift in hosting, support and development model
  • APS Strategic Commissioning Framework target to reduce 2 core ICT contractor roles in 2025-26 and transition enabling ICT from outsourced to a hybrid in-house/managed-service model
  • Exploration of AI and large language models for data insights, research impact reporting and evaluation
  • Rising cybersecurity risk and increasing demands around data access, sharing, transparency and open science
  • Government reform initiatives on integrity, fraud and corruption control and APS capability
  • Sector pressure from record-low grant success rates and contested peer-review fairness

Challenges & risks

  • Financial sustainability of operations and project investment — named capability priority and an enterprise risk on fiscal control
  • Sapphire/ICT solutions operating suboptimally or increasing stakeholder burden (rated Moderate tolerance — the highest in the risk table)
  • Cyber and information-security threats to ICT platforms and data
  • Grant application/review processes being an unreasonable burden on researchers and peer reviewers
  • Research misconduct, fraud or corruption in funded research not adequately managed; foreign interference in NHMRC-funded research
  • Workforce capability and retention — reliance on a suitably skilled workforce while insourcing core work under the Commissioning Framework
  • Maintaining community trust in science amid misinformation and contested funding fairness
  • Dependence on third-party providers for business outcomes

In the news & controversies

  • NHMRC's Ideas Grants success rate fell to ~8.1% (190 of 2,347 funded), down from 10.1% the prior year; peak bodies (AAMRI, ASMR) publicly criticised the system's impact on careers and researcher wellbeing while NHMRC acknowledged concern over low funded rates despite a record ~A$280m round. (Research Professional News, 'Success rate for NHMRC Ideas Grants dips below 10 per cent' — https://www.researchprofessionalnews.com/rr-news-australia-government-agencies-2026-1-success-rate-for-nhmrc-ideas-grants-dips-below-10-per-cent/)
    A live, politically sensitive pressure point — NHMRC must defend the integrity and efficiency of its allocation model, opening advisory work on process redesign, sector-impact analysis, evaluation and stakeholder engagement.
  • Academic analysis argued NHMRC's 'application-centric' peer-review model (≈5 independent reviewers, no deliberating panels) is vulnerable to assessor bias, conflicts of interest and statistical noise, and that real per-capita funding had fallen materially over a decade. (The Conversation, 'More money and smarter choices: how to fix Australia's broken NHMRC medical research funding system' (Aug 2022) — https://theconversation.com/more-money-and-smarter-choices-how-to-fix-australias-broken-nhmrc-medical-research-funding-system-188003)
    Recognised methodological weaknesses in scoring and peer review create scope for evaluation-design, data-analytics and assurance support — NHMRC was already commissioning peer-review reform advice.
  • Long-running criticism that the assessment process disadvantages younger and female researchers and rewards safe, established projects; NHMRC has acknowledged shortcomings and undertaken structural review of the grant program. (The Conversation, 'Is the NHMRC funding process fair?' — https://theconversation.com/is-the-nhmrc-funding-process-fair-67778)
    Equity, diversity and program-design reform is a recurring theme aligning with NHMRC's 'Embed equity' and 'Maintain excellence in peer review' priorities — relevant for policy evaluation and equity-impact analysis.

ANAO audit findings

  • ANAO performance audit 'Administration of Grants by the NHMRC' found shortcomings significantly impacting effectiveness — inconsistent application of guidelines including conflict-of-interest provisions, poor post-award compliance and acquittal backlogs, unsystematic debt recovery, and grants-management systems that did not adequately support administration or outcome reporting (data ~2009; dated). (ANAO, 'Administration of Grants by the National Health and Medical Research Council' — https://www.anao.gov.au/work/performance-audit/administration-grants-the-national-health-and-medical-research-council)
    Conflict-of-interest controls, grants acquittals/assurance and grants-management-system capability are documented historical weak points — durable, credible themes for assurance and Digital/ICT work, though years of remediation may have occurred.
  • An ANAO performance audit on NHMRC's fraud-control arrangements exists; publication date and detailed findings were not confirmed in the research. (ANAO, 'Fraud Control Arrangements in the NHMRC' — https://www.anao.gov.au/work/performance-audit/fraud-control-arrangements-the-national-health-and-medical-research-council)
    Indicates fraud control/integrity has been an audit focus — a potential governance/assurance line; currency should be verified before relying on it.
  • NHMRC was a designated entity in Auditor-General Report No.46 of 2019-20 (25 June 2020) examining whether Education and Health portfolio entities had appropriately implemented agreed ANAO and parliamentary-committee recommendations. (ANAO, 'Implementation of ANAO and Parliamentary Committee Recommendations — Education and Health Portfolios' — https://www.anao.gov.au/work/performance-audit/implementation-anao-parliamentary-committee-recommendations-education-health-portfolios)
    Shows NHMRC is in scope for ANAO follow-up on recommendation implementation — relevant to audit-remediation tracking and governance uplift (note: 2020, just outside the 3-year window).
