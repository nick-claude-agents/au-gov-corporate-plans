Australian Skills Quality Authority (ASQA)
Employment and Workplace Relations · Corporate Plan 2025-26 (covering 1 July 2025 to 30 June 2029) · BD confidence: High

ASQA is the national VET regulator, a non-corporate Commonwealth entity of ~250 staff, entering a pivotal reform year as the revised Standards for RTOs 2025 take effect from 1 July 2025. The plan's dominant signals are: implementing and supporting sector transition to the new Standards; an escalating integrity/fraud agenda (Integrity Unit, Tip-off line, 'cash for quals' and CRICOS phoenixing); a multi-year Digital Transformation Program replacing legacy systems and uplifting data analytics; and a maturing risk-based regulatory model including a dual-sector strategy with TEQSA and a course-accreditation delegation pilot. External pressure is real and acute — large-scale qualification fraud, a CRICOS registration freeze, an ANAO finding that ASQA was 'not yet fully effective' at planning/implementing reform, and a new Cost Recovery Implementation Statement for 2026-27. As a small regulator under simultaneous reform, integrity and digital pressures, ASQA presents credible demand for assurance, program delivery, data/analytics, costing and workforce capability work — best pitched as proportionate, expertise-led engagements.

Strategic priorities

  • Implement the revised Standards for RTOs 2025 — Lead the sector's transition to the new outcomes-focused Standards taking effect 1 July 2025, providing guidance, resources, communications and consultation while maintaining compliance and enforcement where expectations are not met.
  • Protect sector integrity against fraud and bad-faith operators — Expand Integrity Unit work, the Tip-off line and networked intelligence to identify and disrupt 'cash for quals' schemes, RPL exploitation, phoenixing and non-genuine providers, including participation in the Fraud Fusion Taskforce and Operation Inglenook.
  • Deliver the Digital Transformation Program — Move regulatory operations off inadequate legacy IT, modernise digital infrastructure, deliver a refreshed website and new portal functionality, and uplift data analytics for provider-risk targeting and real-time decision-making.
  • Mature a risk-based regulatory model and reduce duplication — Differentiate regulatory supervision by provider risk profile, implement a dual-sector strategy with TEQSA (joint audits, information sharing, harmonised requirements), and evaluate a pilot delegating course accreditation to TAFEs and a dual-sector provider.
  • Redevelop the cost recovery model — Consult with stakeholders and State and Territory Skills Ministers to develop a new Cost Recovery Implementation Statement for 2026-27 better aligned to regulatory practice and level of supervision.
  • Build workforce capability and culture — Implement the Workforce Plan 2025-28 and Capability Framework aligned to the APS Workforce Strategy, including building regulatory capability and adoption of AI.

Reform & change drivers

  • Revised Standards for RTOs 2025 effective 1 July 2025 — a 'step change' requiring significant maturing across the VET system
  • National Skills Agreement (five-year Commonwealth/state) and Australian Universities Accord driving a whole-of-system, integrated VET/higher-education/migration approach
  • Ministerial Statement of Expectations and the Regulator Performance Guide (RMG 128) setting performance and reporting expectations
  • Government integrity investment funding the Integrity Unit and Tip-off line; participation in Fraud Fusion Taskforce and Operation Inglenook
  • Managed-growth/sustainability measures for international education and CRICOS registration suspension under the Education Legislation Amendment (Integrity and Other Measures) Act 2025
  • Ongoing implementation of recommendations from the Rapid Review of ASQA's regulatory practices
  • New Cost Recovery Implementation Statement required for 2026-27
  • Refreshed Strategic Objectives and KPIs (May 2025) and a maturing performance framework

Challenges & risks

  • Stated strategic risk that ASQA does not attract, support and retain an engaged, skilled and resilient workforce — acute for a 250-person expertise-heavy regulator
  • Strategic risk that ASQA does not meet performance targets and compliance obligations, with the DTP named as a key control
  • Strategic risk that ASQA does not effectively address risks to VET quality and integrity amid escalating fraud threats
  • Legacy IT systems explicitly described as 'inadequate', creating delivery and data risk
  • Pressure to deliver key projects and programs on schedule (KPI 3B) while simultaneously running reform, integrity and digital programs
  • Hostile private-provider sentiment over the CRICOS freeze and 'resourcing the regulator' critique
  • ANAO finding that ASQA was 'not yet fully effective' at planning and implementing reform — execution credibility risk

In the news & controversies

  • ASQA is running an ongoing program cancelling registrations and qualifications of fraudulent/poor-quality providers across early childhood, aged care, disability, construction and automotive, supported by $4.7m surge funding in 2025-26 and a further $4.8m in 2026-27 (partly cost-recovered) to respond to large-scale fraudulent qualification issuance. (Department of Employment and Workplace Relations — https://www.dewr.gov.au/skills-and-training/asqa-regulatory-action-cancelled-vet-qualifications)
    A live integrity crisis with dedicated new funding signals near-term demand for enforcement/investigation surge capacity, risk analytics and provider-remediation support.
  • From 19 May new CRICOS registration applications to ASQA (new providers and new courses for existing VET/ELICOS providers) are suspended until 19 May 2027 under the Education Legislation Amendment (Integrity and Other Measures) Act 2025; private providers have criticised the measure, arguing government should properly resource the regulator rather than target independent providers. (The PIE News — https://thepienews.com/australia-freezes-new-overseas-student-provider-registrations/)
    The 'resource the regulator' critique points to application-assessment throughput and capacity pressure at ASQA, and a hostile stakeholder base — openings for process design, capacity advice and stakeholder engagement support.

ANAO audit findings

  • The ANAO performance audit 'Effectiveness of Planning and Implementation of Reform by the Australian Skills Quality Authority' concluded ASQA was not yet fully effective in planning and implementing its VET-regulation reform (triggered by the 2020 Rapid Review); ASQA agreed to all four recommendations. (Australian National Audit Office — https://www.anao.gov.au/work/performance-audit/effectiveness-planning-and-implementation-reform-the-australian-skills-quality-authority)
    Documented weaknesses in program/risk management and reporting with agreed recommendations create recurring demand for implementation assurance, governance uplift and corporate-planning support — directly relevant as the Standards reform now ramps up.
