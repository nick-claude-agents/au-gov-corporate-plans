Strategic priorities

  • Deliver and implement the Murray–Darling Basin Plan — MDBA's core statutory task is implementing the Basin Plan 2012 and meeting water-recovery and review milestones, which the corporate plan (per the landing page) frames as collaborative work with governments, communities, industry and researchers over a four-year horizon.
  • River operations and water management — Day-to-day operation of the River Murray system and management of shared water resources under the Basin Agreement — an activity currently exposed to operational-risk litigation (Barmah–Millewa).
  • Statutory compliance and Basin Plan review — Discharging responsibilities under the Water Act 2007 and preparing for/responding to Basin Plan evaluation and review obligations.

Reform & change drivers

  • Restoring Our Rivers Act and amended Basin Plan settings extending water-recovery timelines to 2027, creating new delivery and accountability pressures
  • Climate variability and drought cycles increasing demand for hydrological modelling and water-management decision support
  • Sustained political and parliamentary scrutiny of Basin Plan delivery milestones
  • Legal exposure from river-operations decisions (Barmah–Millewa Forest overbank transfers)

Challenges & risks

  • Basin Plan delivery-timeline risk under intense political scrutiny in Senate Estimates
  • Active class-action litigation over river-operations and Barmah–Millewa Forest management, raising operational and reputational risk
  • Maintaining stakeholder and community trust across competing state, industry and environmental interests
  • Defensibility of technical/hydrological decisions given Estimates and discovery exposure
  • Note: enterprise risks cannot be confirmed because the substantive plan text was not supplied
