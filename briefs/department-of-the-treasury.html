Department of the Treasury
Treasury · Corporate Plan 2025-26 · BD confidence: Medium

Treasury is the Commonwealth's lead economic adviser — a policy-department archetype where demand sits in governance, evaluation, workforce, data/digital and assurance rather than large service-delivery programs. The dominant theme for 2025-26 is absorbing significant machinery-of-government changes (housing supply, homelessness, construction, cities and planning policy embedded into Treasury via the 13 May 2025 AAO), which creates operating-model, workforce and performance-framework demand. The plan signals deliberate uplift in data/AI capability (a second Enterprise Data Strategy, a refreshed Digital and Cyber Security Strategy), an evaluation push through the Australian Centre for Evaluation, and continued embedding of a risk framework and pro-integrity culture. External signals reinforce this: ANAO recommendations on gifts/benefits, an in-progress HAFF audit, and a Measuring What Matters framework audit. Note the plan commits to bringing some ICT/digital work in-house under the APS Strategic Commissioning Framework, tempering pure build opportunities.

Strategic priorities

  • Integrate new housing and related functions — Treasury must operationalise a range of new housing functions (plus homelessness, construction, cities and planning policy) embedded through 2025 machinery-of-government changes, and deliver the National Housing Accord with Housing Australia.
  • Streamline and strengthen the foreign investment framework — Treasury is moving to a risk-based foreign investment model — faster decisions on low-risk proposals (50% within 30 days), more scrutiny of high-risk proposals, and stronger compliance monitoring and enforcement.
  • Uplift data, digital, AI and cyber capability — Treasury will refresh its 2024-26 Digital and Cyber Security Strategy, launch a second Enterprise Data Strategy, and integrate AI guidance and governance into existing strategies in 2025-26.
  • Build a service-wide evaluation capability — Through the Australian Centre for Evaluation, Treasury will release the Treasury Evaluation Strategy and oversee a service-wide evaluation capability uplift to embed evidence in decision-making.
  • Embed risk management and pro-integrity culture — Treasury will continue embedding its risk framework (finalised late 2024), integrate climate risk into governance, and embed the Integrity Strategy 2024-2028 with a focus on conflict-of-interest management.
  • Reform the Payment Times Reporting Scheme — Treasury is overhauling the Payment Times Reporting Scheme following the statutory review, with new reporting requirements, IT system and a re-established compliance target.
  • Refresh the corporate performance framework — Treasury introduced three new performance measures for 2025-26 and will review the framework for completeness to incorporate transferred functions, potentially varying the Corporate Plan.

Reform & change drivers

  • Machinery-of-government changes (13 May 2025 AAO) embedding housing, homelessness, construction, cities and planning functions into Treasury
  • Government housing agenda: National Housing Accord, Housing Australia Future Fund (40,000 dwellings to 2029), National Housing Supply and Affordability Council
  • Foreign investment framework reform with new 30-day decision target and risk-based triage from 1 January 2025
  • Statutory review response overhauling the Payment Times Reporting Scheme, including a new IT system
  • Future Made in Australia: National Interest Framework, Investor Front Door pilot, net zero and economic-resilience industrial priorities
  • Net zero transition: whole-of-economy modelling for the Net Zero Plan and Sustainable Finance Roadmap
  • Economic Reform Roundtable focused on productivity, resilience and budget sustainability
  • APS Strategic Commissioning Framework driving in-housing of some ICT and digital core work
  • Commonwealth Climate Risk and Opportunity Management Program requiring enterprise climate risk assessment
  • Rapid AI adoption requiring governance, data capability and responsible-use frameworks

Challenges & risks

  • Absorbing new functions and staff through MoG changes while maintaining advice quality (named staff capability and culture risks)
  • Modelling and analytical capability risk in a rapidly changing economic environment including net zero transition
  • Housing policy and program delivery risk using novel partnership delivery arrangements
  • IT systems, cyber security, governance and capability risk (very low to medium tolerance)
  • Integrity and conflict-of-interest exposure, reinforced by an ANAO gifts/benefits audit with four agreed recommendations
  • Performance framework gaps as new measures bed in and transferred functions are incorporated
  • Productivity and budget-sustainability pressures shaping the policy workload
  • Weak/maturing regulator stakeholder satisfaction (65% target retained after consecutive misses)

ANAO audit findings

  • ANAO found Treasury 'largely effective' in complying with gifts, benefits and hospitality requirements but identified shortcomings in policy alignment with APSC requirements, training for statutory office holders, and detection processes; four recommendations were made and all agreed. (ANAO — https://www.anao.gov.au/work/performance-audit/compliance-gifts-benefits-and-hospitality-requirements-the-department-of-the-treasury)
    Open remediation runway in integrity controls, policy alignment and APS-conduct training design — discrete, scoped advisory work.
  • ANAO Auditor-General Report No. 34 of 2024-25 assessed the effectiveness of Treasury's design and implementation of the Measuring What Matters national wellbeing framework released in July 2023. (ANAO — https://www.anao.gov.au/work/performance-audit/treasurys-design-and-implementation-of-the-measuring-what-matters-framework)
    Scrutiny of Treasury's measurement/evaluation capability — potential demand for performance-measurement, data and framework-implementation support.
  • ANAO has an in-progress performance audit assessing the effectiveness of Treasury's design and delivery of the Housing Australia Future Fund. (ANAO — https://www.anao.gov.au/work-program/in-progress)
    An imminent audit on a flagship program; pre- and post-audit assurance, program-design and delivery-remediation work is likely to follow.
  • In the ANAO's 2022-23 annual performance statements audits, Treasury received an unmodified (clean) conclusion while four other departments were qualified. (ANAO — https://www.anao.gov.au/work/performance-statements-audit/audits-of-the-annual-performance-statements-of-australian-government-entities-2022-23)
    Treasury's reporting baseline is comparatively sound, so opportunities lean toward continuous improvement rather than crisis remediation.

Parliamentary matters

  • The Treasury portfolio was examined across recent Senate Economics Legislation Committee estimates rounds, including 2023-24 Additional Estimates, 2024-25 Budget Estimates and 2024-25 Supplementary Budget Estimates (with the then-Secretary tabling an opening statement). (Parliament of Australia — https://www.aph.gov.au/Parliamentary_Business/Senate_estimates/Economics/2024-25_Budget_estimates)
