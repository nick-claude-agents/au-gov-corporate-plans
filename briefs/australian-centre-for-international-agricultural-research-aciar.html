Australian Centre for International Agricultural Research (ACIAR)
Foreign Affairs and Trade · Corporate Plan 2025-26 · BD confidence: Medium

ACIAR is a small (≈90 staff), expertise-heavy non-corporate Commonwealth entity that brokers and funds collaborative international agricultural research-for-development across the Indo-Pacific and Africa, funded predominantly from Australia's ODA appropriation. Its agenda for 2025-26 is steady-state in mission but carries genuine corporate-change signals: a first HQ relocation in 30 years (to Forrest, ACT, with a net-zero commitment), continued investment in its integrated ICT platform that unifies research/partnership/corporate/compliance data, and a named strategic risk around maintaining a sustainable funding model. With cybersecurity, people safety, relationship management and research disruption explicitly listed as enterprise risks, the most credible Consulting Firm openings are in enabling/corporate functions — ICT and data, cyber, governance/risk uplift, performance-measure design and financial sustainability — rather than large program delivery. Budgets are modest, so offerings must be right-sized to short advisory and framework engagements.

Strategic priorities

  • Climate-smart food security and resilience — Concentrate investment in climate-smart agriculture and strengthening resilience across agriculture, fisheries and forestry systems in partner countries.
  • Capacity development through fellowships and local institutions — Build scientific and policy capability via long-term partnerships, fellowships (JAF, JDF, MWF, PASS) and ACIAR Learn, and engage alumni networks.
  • Locally led, partner-driven investment — Ensure investment decisions are locally led and co-designed through the in-country Country Network across 11 diplomatic-mission locations.
  • Science diplomacy and broadened partnerships — Strengthen Australia's role as a trusted regional partner and broaden engagement with governments, private sector and development partners, including expansion into northern and western Africa.
  • Strengthening strategic risk and business planning — Enhance integration of strategic risk reporting into business planning and expand risk training and outreach across the organisation in 2025-26.

Reform & change drivers

  • Headquarters relocation to new offices in Forrest, ACT — first move in 30 years — aligned to the Government's net zero commitment
  • Alignment of investments with Australia's International Development Policy and the UN 2030 SDGs
  • Named strategic risk: failure to maintain a sustainable funding model (ODA-dependent appropriation)
  • Continued build-out of an integrated ICT platform unifying research, partnership, corporate and compliance data
  • Operating under the ACIAR 10-Year Strategy 2018-2027, approaching its final years (likely strategy refresh ahead)
  • Geographic expansion into northern and western Africa, building on eastern/southern Africa partnerships

Challenges & risks

  • Cybersecurity listed as a top strategic risk for a data- and partnership-intensive agency operating across many jurisdictions
  • Major failure(s) of ICT systems identified as a strategic risk
  • People safety risk across operational environments in fragile/conflict-affected regions
  • Disruption to research programs from global, national and local events
  • Failure to maintain a sustainable funding model given ODA appropriation dependence
  • Failure to engage key stakeholders/partnerships on government expectations and policies
  • Failure to have appropriate governance structures in place
  • Small workforce (≈90 staff) carrying a globally dispersed, complex portfolio — capability concentration risk
