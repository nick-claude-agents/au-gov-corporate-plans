Australian Research Council (ARC)
Education · Corporate Plan 2025-26 · BD confidence: High

The ARC is a small, expertise-heavy non-corporate Commonwealth entity that administers the ~$1bn/year National Competitive Grants Program (NCGP) and provides research policy advice. FY2025-26 is a once-in-a-generation reform year: the Board is redesigning the entire NCGP off the back of its Policy Review, building a new 10-year ARC Strategy, and developing a new data-driven National Research Evaluation and Impact Framework in response to the Universities Accord. This reform agenda touches every stage of the funding cycle (assessment, post-award, evaluation) and is underpinned by data/analytics uplift, the RMS platform, a new information management system, cyber and privacy improvements, and an office relocation. The agency also operates under live external pressure — recurring ministerial-veto/research-security controversy, ANAO interest in grants administration, and the new governance regime following the ARC Act amendments — making independent assurance, program delivery and data work credible (if modestly sized) BD prospects. Note the plan was supplied as the 2025-26 plan despite a 2024-25 source URL.

Strategic priorities

  • Redesign and implement a revitalised NCGP — Provide government with recommendations for a simpler NCGP and then transition to a new model from 2026, rethinking the assessment model, post-award grant management and evaluation across the whole funding cycle.
  • Deliver a 10-year ARC Strategy — Develop a long-term strategy to guide the ~$1bn annual NCGP investment, align with national priorities and provide stewardship for a diverse, thriving research sector.
  • Build a National Research Evaluation and Impact Framework — Develop a modern, data-driven framework to assess the excellence, impact and depth of university research, responding to the Universities Accord recommendation for intelligent-technology-based evaluation; FY2025-26 deliverable is a proposal.
  • Strengthen research integrity and security — Continue work with the Department of Education, NHMRC and national security agencies to safeguard research integrity and protect Australian research from foreign interference risks.
  • Develop enhanced data, analytics and communication capabilities — Build data/analytics capability to better understand the research landscape, evaluate NCGP performance and tell the story of ARC-funded research; uplift data literacy per the ARC Data Strategy.
  • Modernise technology and corporate infrastructure — Maintain and adapt RMS, implement a new information management system, optimise the website, improve cyber security, uplift privacy maturity, and relocate to new premises.

Reform & change drivers

  • NCGP Policy Review final report due to government Q3 2025, with progressive transition to a new funding model from 2026
  • Australian Universities Accord Final Report response driving a new, data-driven research evaluation approach
  • Revised Australian Research Council Act 2001 and new ARC Board/governance arrangements (post ARC Act amendment legislation)
  • Establishment of the Australian Tertiary Education Commission and the Strategic Examination of Research and Development reshaping the sector
  • Heightened foreign-interference and research-security risk environment
  • Growth of AI creating new opportunities and risks for ARC operations and the research sector
  • APS Strategic Commissioning Framework pressure to reduce outsourcing of core program/project management work

Challenges & risks

  • Small agency with 'big ambition' delivering a major multi-year reform with constrained resources, requiring reprioritisation
  • Reported job cuts (Dec 2025) potentially reducing internal delivery capacity at the height of reform
  • Strategic risk of failing to provide strong leadership on research strategy
  • Strategic risk that ARC fails to comply with legislative requirements and key government policies
  • Strategic risk of not engaging effectively with diverse stakeholders
  • Legacy/critical dependence on the RMS platform that must be adapted to support a redesigned NCGP
  • Privacy maturity and cyber security explicitly flagged as needing uplift
  • APS Strategic Commissioning Framework limits reliance on contractors for core program/project management — a constraint on certain engagement types

In the news & controversies

  • Ministerial veto of six peer-reviewed ARC grants on 'national interest' grounds (Dec 2021) triggered sustained sector backlash and College of Experts resignations, with fallout continuing into late 2024. (InnovationAus; The Conversation; The Mandarin — https://www.innovationaus.com/minister-rejects-six-peer-reviewed-arc-research-grants-on-national-interest-grounds/)
    Grant-integrity, governance and independent process-assurance work is a live demand area; lead with independence and probity credibility.
  • In 2025 a Labor minister again declined/vetoed ARC grants on national-security grounds, reportedly causing confusion and delays despite reform; ARC issued an 'Update on Research Security'. (Times Higher Education; Research Professional News; ARC — https://www.arc.gov.au/news-and-publications/media/update-research-security)
    National-interest/security screening of research is an active operational pain point — scope for due-diligence and process-design advisory around research security frameworks.
  • Australian Research Council job cuts reported on 4 December 2025. (The Canberra Times — https://www.canberratimes.com.au/story/9127079/australian-research-council-job-cuts-revealed/)
    Workforce reduction during a major reform may increase reliance on external delivery and assurance support, or signal tighter external spend — clarify early in any approach.
