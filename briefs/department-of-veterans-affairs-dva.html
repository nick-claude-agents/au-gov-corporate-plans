Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA)
Defence · Corporate Plan 2025-26 · BD confidence: High

DVA is a large service-delivery agency in the midst of the most significant reform period in its history, driven by the 122 recommendations of the Royal Commission into Defence and Veteran Suicide (104 agreed/agreed-in-principle) and the implementation of major new legislation (the VETS Act) that closes the VEA and DRCA to new claims from 1 July 2026 in favour of a single MRCA. DVA is lead/joint lead for 41 recommendations, including standing up a new agency focused on veteran wellbeing, a national ex-service organisation peak body, and the National Veterans' Data Asset — all multi-year, complex, interdependent programs. The plan candidly admits its digital ecosystem rests on legacy ICT (financial management system and Open Arms clinical case management being replaced in 2025-26), that claims volumes keep growing, and that it is rebuilding data governance, privacy practice and social licence. This is a deep, multi-year pipeline for program delivery, assurance, data, digital build and change management, tempered by the APS Strategic Commissioning Framework push to bring core work in-house.

Strategic priorities

  • Implement the Royal Commission response — Implementing the Government's response to the Royal Commission's 122 recommendations is the department's core multi-year priority, spanning culture, systems, claims processing, transition, data/research, healthcare and workforce. DVA is lead or joint lead for 41 recommendations, overseen by the Royal Commission Implementation Committee (RCIC).
  • Stand up a new veteran wellbeing agency and ESO peak body — Subject to Government decisions following co-design, DVA is working to establish a new agency focused on veteran wellbeing (Rec 87) and a national peak body for ex-service organisations (Rec 89) — a fundamental structural shift in how it interacts with and supports veterans.
  • Implement the VETS Act legislative harmonisation — Embed the Veterans' Entitlements, Treatment and Support (Simplification and Harmonisation) Act 2025. From 1 July 2026 the VEA and DRCA close to new claims and all claims are determined under an enhanced MRCA, requiring ICT changes, advocate/employee training and community communication.
  • Establish the National Veterans' Data Asset and modern data capability — Build a modern enterprise data warehouse and analytics capability, establish the National Veterans' Data Asset and National Veteran Indicator (Rec 107), uplift data governance and literacy, and resolve legislative data-sharing barriers with Defence.
  • Modernise the digital ecosystem and adopt AI responsibly — Replace legacy ICT including the financial management system (2025-26) and the Open Arms clinical case management solution (2025-26), deliver new digital and data strategies, and pilot AI (Copilot trial, AI website search, an AI prototyping environment with Finance) under the Commonwealth's responsible AI policy.
  • Improve claims timeliness and the client experience — Sustain the cleared unallocated claims backlog and lift performance, raising claims-processing timeliness targets to >90%, with initiatives around online lodgement and streamlined handling of complex claims.

Reform & change drivers

  • Royal Commission into Defence and Veteran Suicide Final Report (Sep 2024) — 122 recommendations, Government response Dec 2024; DVA lead/joint lead for 41
  • VETS Act 2025 — closes VEA and DRCA to new claims from 1 July 2026, consolidating onto an enhanced MRCA
  • Establishment of the Defence and Veterans' Services Commission (DVSC) as independent oversight body from 29 September 2025
  • Continuing growth in claims volumes increasing downstream service pressure
  • Legacy ICT systems requiring replacement (financial management system; Open Arms clinical case management)
  • APS Strategic Commissioning Framework — pressure to bring core work in-house and reduce reliance on contractors
  • ANAO audit findings cited as a driver of the redesigned performance framework
  • APSC Capability Review of DVA (finalised October 2024) confirming governance trajectory but urging continued maturation
  • Joint Defence/DVA strategies: Family Wellbeing Strategy 2025-2030 and Mental Health and Wellbeing Strategy 2025-2030
  • Additional $47.6m in 2025-26 to maintain service-delivery resourcing

Challenges & risks

  • Claims processing efficiency and timeliness under continuing demand growth — named enterprise risk 'Managing claims efficiently and effectively'
  • Legacy ICT modernisation risk while delivering simultaneous large reform programs
  • Complex interdependencies between Royal Commission recommendations (e.g. new agency, data asset, transition supports) creating delivery sequencing risk
  • Privacy, data integrity and rebuilding 'social licence and trust' with the veteran community — enterprise risk 'Operational Integrity'
  • Workforce capability, recruitment and retention pressures while bringing core work in-house under APS reform
  • Legislative data-sharing barriers between DVA and Defence impeding the National Veterans' Data Asset
  • Transition risk around the 1 July 2026 VETS Act cutover affecting advocates, employees and claimants
  • Newly introduced performance measures (many ≥80% indicative, monitored first year) — unproven and under public scrutiny
  • Public criticism over claim wait-time reporting transparency
