Australian War Memorial (AWM)
Veterans' Affairs · Corporate Plan 2024-25 (covering 2024-25 to 2027-28) · BD confidence: Medium

The AWM is a cultural/collecting institution in the midst of a once-in-a-generation $595m+ Development Project ('Our Continuing Story'), with phase-one new spaces (Southern Entry, Charles Bean Research Centre) transitioning into operations in 2024-25 and full completion by 2028. The dominant BD signals are the Transition Project (a new operating model, organisational structural review and change management to absorb 5,500m2 of new gallery space), genuine financial-sustainability pressure (a forecast $14.6m operating loss, 87% appropriation-dependent, with a mandate to grow non-government revenue), and a heavy digital/collections agenda (Digital Engagement Strategy, ICT Strategic Plan, the Memorial Enterprise Transformation (MET) Program, and large-scale digitisation). The APS Strategic Commissioning Framework also pushes AWM to reduce contractor/outsourced core work, so engagements should be framed as capability transfer rather than long-term staff substitution. This is a smaller-budget entity, so offerings must be proportionate: targeted advisory, framework design, evaluation, and discrete digital/data delivery rather than large embedded programs — with the notable exception of assurance and transition support for the major capital project.

Strategic priorities

  • Deliver and transition the Development Project — Complete phase one (Central Energy Plant, Southern Entry, Charles Bean Research Centre) and progress Anzac Hall/Glazed Link, while transitioning new functions and capabilities into live operations and integrating deliverables via the Transition Program.
  • Build a sustainable operating model — Run an organisational structural review and revised operating model that aligns revenue, services and structure to run a much larger post-development Memorial; reduce outsourcing of core work per the APS Strategic Commissioning Framework.
  • Grow and diversify non-government revenue — Increase commercial (retail, F&B, ecommerce, licensing, events) and philanthropic income to offset a constrained fiscal position and forecast operating losses, leveraging new facilities.
  • Digital access and collection modernisation — Deliver the Digital Engagement Strategy and ICT Strategic Plan, enhance collections systems/repository, avoid technology obsolescence, and digitise at-risk and high-use items for online access.
  • Care for and preserve the National Collection — Acquire, conserve, digitise and store the collection per the Collection Development, Preservation and Hazard Management Plans, including Treloar Complex storage and logistics planning.

Reform & change drivers

  • The $498.7m Development Project (plus a $50m variation and $47m ancillary investment) with a hard 30 June 2028 completion date — the largest change since 1941.
  • Transition Project (established 2022) to design a sustainable post-development operating model and absorb a 5,500m2 gallery expansion.
  • APS Strategic Commissioning Framework driving a reduction in outsourced/contractor core work (Trades & Labour, Administration, Data & Research) — including a planned reduction of seven contractor positions.
  • Financial pressure: forecast $14.6m operating loss in 2024-25, ongoing losses, 87% appropriation dependence, and unfunded asset-base depreciation, with ongoing operational funding still to be sought for expanded gallery space.
  • Digital Engagement Strategy and 2023-2028 ICT Strategic Plan, plus the Memorial Enterprise Transformation (MET) Program.
  • Demographic/audience shift: need to connect with younger visitors, recent veterans and post-pandemic interstate/international visitors (future audiences market research underway).

Challenges & risks

  • Development Project may fail to deliver expected infrastructure/gallery outcomes against program and budget.
  • Inability to meet service levels through a period of significant change; disruption and competing priorities between project delivery and live operations.
  • Loss of key personnel, resourcing gaps and difficulty attracting/retaining staff in a tight labour market, plus pay-fragmentation issues.
  • Financial sustainability: structural operating losses and over-reliance on appropriation in a constrained fiscal environment.
  • Collections management complexity — preservation, digitisation and accessibility risks, plus complicated/ageing collection management systems and hazardous-material handling.
  • Failure to connect with young visitors and recent veterans, or to meet community expectations for commemorations and exhibitions.

  • High Approach the Director's office and the COO/CFO function with a financial-sustainability and revenue-diversification proposition tailored to the $14.6m operating loss and the post-development funding case.
    Financial sustainability is an explicit, board-level pressure with hard numbers; a proportionate review supports the operational-funding bid AWM says it must make to government for expanded gallery space.
  • High Offer the Transition Program team a target operating model / structural-review and change-management capability statement, framed around insourcing core work under the APS Strategic Commissioning Framework.
    The structural review and operating-model work are named, time-bound and central to 2024-25; framing as capability transfer (not contractor substitution) aligns with AWM's commitment to reduce outsourcing.
  • High Pitch independent, gateway-style delivery assurance to the Development Project / Transition Program governance (Council audit & risk committee and project SRO) ahead of each phase handover.
    The Memorial's number-one strategic risk is delivery failure of a $595m+ project with a fixed 2028 deadline; independent assurance is low-sensitivity and timely as phase-one functions transition into operations in 2024-25.
  • Medium Prepare a short thought piece on collections-repository modernisation and digitisation delivery for the Digital Engagement / ICT leadership, referencing the MET Program and ICT Strategic Plan objectives.
    Digital and collections modernisation is a recurring multi-year theme; an early, specific point of view positions the firm for discovery and build work as capital is released in stages.

Talking points

  • You are transitioning phase-one functions into live operations in 2024-25 — independent gateway assurance at each handover de-risks the single biggest item on your enterprise risk register.
  • A forecast $14.6m operating loss and a mandate to grow commercial and philanthropic revenue point to a tight, proportionate financial-sustainability review that also strengthens your operational-funding case to government.
  • Your structural review and revised operating model need a target operating model that sizes the workforce for 5,500m2 of new gallery space — designed to insource core work in line with the APS Strategic Commissioning Framework, not entrench contractors.
  • Collections repository modernisation and automated digital access are multi-year ambitions — we can help scope discovery and then build and integrate, not just advise.
  • Future-audiences research is only valuable if it converts to an implementation strategy with measurable engagement outcomes — we can connect the research to a data-informed delivery plan.
