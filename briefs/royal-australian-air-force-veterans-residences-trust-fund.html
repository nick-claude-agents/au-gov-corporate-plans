Royal Australian Air Force Veterans' Residences Trust Fund
Defence · Corporate Plan 2024-25 (four-year outlook 2024-28) · BD confidence: Low

The RAAF Veterans' Residences Trust is a very small WWII-legacy Corporate Commonwealth Entity governed by volunteer trustees and run by just two staff (one full-time, one part-time). It is a self-funding niche provider of ~76 rental residences across five capital cities for ex-RAAF personnel in necessitous circumstances. The plan is deliberately minimalist: it explicitly states no need for a technology strategy, no infrastructure expansion, and no cooperation requirements beyond the RSL/AFA/RAAF for tenant and trustee nominations. From a BD perspective the addressable demand is extremely thin and budget is constrained by statute; the only tangible signals are three named initiatives — amending the 1953 Act to broaden accommodation-related support, developing a strategic asset management plan, and improving stakeholder engagement to lift eligible-tenant numbers. Any Consulting Firm engagement here would be a small, one-off advisory at most, not a sustained account.

Strategic priorities

  • Develop a strategic asset management plan — The Trust has committed to developing and implementing a strategic asset management plan for its portfolio of rental properties across Brisbane, Sydney, Melbourne, Adelaide and Hobart, led by trustee Tindal.
  • Amend the enabling 1953 Act to broaden support — The Trust seeks legislative amendment to allow it to provide a wider range of accommodation-related support to eligible persons, beyond simply providing a residence, led by trustee Schiller.
  • Increase eligible tenant numbers via stakeholder engagement — The Trust aims to increase the number of eligible tenants, primarily through improved engagement with the Air Force Association and the RSL.
  • Maintain financial viability within statutory constraints — As a self-funding entity, the Trust must remain solvent and may allocate accommodation to non-eligible persons or the general public at market rates to preserve viability.

Reform & change drivers

  • Proposed amendment to the RAAF Veterans' Residences Trust Act 1953 to broaden the Trust's permissible support activities
  • Need to maintain financial viability as a self-funding entity under statutory constraints
  • Dependence on the Department of Defence for office accommodation and ICT infrastructure support
  • Reliance on volunteer trustees and a two-person staff, creating key-person and continuity risk

Challenges & risks

  • Very small workforce (two employees) with limited surge capacity beyond business-as-usual
  • Continued ability to retain volunteer trustees and qualified staff
  • Maintaining financial viability given statutory constraints on the Trust's activities
  • Ongoing reliance on Department of Defence for infrastructure and ICT
  • Ageing residential property portfolio requiring maintenance to 'modern standards'

ANAO audit findings

  • The Trust is subject to routine ANAO financial-statement audits (e.g. 2020-21); no dedicated ANAO performance audit, adverse finding or recommendation naming the Trust was found. (Transparency Portal Trustees Report 2020-21 — https://www.transparency.gov.au/annual-reports/royal-australian-air-force-veterans-residences-trust-fund/reporting-year/2020-21-4 ; ANAO Defence portfolio — https://www.anao.gov.au/work-program/portfolio/defence)
    There is no audit-driven remediation pipeline to bid into; any advisory need would stem from the trustees' own governance and asset-management decisions rather than external assurance pressure.
