Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS)
Treasury · Corporate Plan 2025-26 · BD confidence: Medium

The ABS is Australia's national statistical agency (a non-corporate Commonwealth entity in the Treasury portfolio), best understood as a scientific/data agency that is capital- and data-intensive and modernising under demand and response-rate pressure. The 2025-26 plan is dominated by two large, time-bound commitments: delivering the 2026 Census of Population and Housing, and the Big Data, Timely Insights (BDTI) program, which is rebuilding core statistical processing in a cloud-based analytics environment and delivering a complete monthly CPI from 26 November 2025. The agency is also reforming data acquisition toward a digital-first model in response to falling survey response rates, and leading the APS Data Profession to build data capability across government. These signals point to demand around program/delivery assurance, digital and data platform build, data integration, cyber/security assurance (especially for the Census), and workforce/capability uplift. The plan text supplied is high-level (a Statistician's message and section headings only), so several opportunities are inferred from named initiatives rather than detailed activity descriptions.

Strategic priorities

  • Deliver a secure, trusted 2026 Census — Finalise preparations through 2025-26 to run the 2026 Census of Population and Housing as an easy and secure digital experience for all Australians.
  • Big Data, Timely Insights (BDTI) modernisation — Rebuild core statistical tools and processes in a cloud-based data analytics environment, enabling a complete monthly CPI measure from 26 November 2025.
  • Modernise data acquisition / digital-first collection — Reform data sourcing to respond to the global long-term decline in survey response rates, creating simple, safe digital experiences and using administrative and alternative data sources.
  • Build data integration capability and partnerships — Partner with government and the private sector to link public and private datasets for policy-relevant research and unlock new data sources.
  • Lead the APS Data Profession — Attract, develop and retain data capabilities of ABS staff and the broader APS by leading the APS Data Profession.

Reform & change drivers

  • 2026 Census delivery deadline — a once-in-five-years national operation with security and public-trust expectations.
  • Complete monthly CPI launch on 26 November 2025 — a hard, externally visible delivery date dependent on BDTI cloud platform readiness.
  • Global long-term decline in survey response rates forcing a shift to administrative and alternative data sources.
  • Cloud migration and modernisation of legacy statistical processing systems under BDTI.
  • Growth in demand for timelier, more granular and integrated data from governments, communities and business.
  • APS-wide data capability uplift mandate as lead of the APS Data Profession.

Challenges & risks

  • Security and public trust around the 2026 Census, given high public sensitivity to data handling and the legacy of prior Census IT incidents.
  • Delivery risk on BDTI and the monthly CPI go-live — fixed date, cloud re-platforming of core economic statistics.
  • Declining survey response rates undermining the quality of traditional collections.
  • Data integration involving linked public/private datasets raises privacy, governance and security exposure.
  • Workforce and data-capability shortages across the APS that the ABS is tasked to address.
  • Modernising legacy systems while maintaining continuity of critical economic and social statistics.
