Australian Taxation Office (ATO)
Treasury · Corporate Plan 2025-26 · BD confidence: High

The ATO's 2025-26 corporate plan (covering the ATO listed entity, including the TPB and ACNC) sets out five enterprise priorities: strengthening payment performance and debt collection, enhancing counter-fraud measures, establishing Payday Super, delivering a digitalised small-business tax experience, and implementing 'Our Performance Evolution' in response to the APSC Capability Review (published March 2025). These are large, time-bound, funded reform programs in a high-volume service-delivery and revenue agency under pressure from a >$50bn collectable-debt position (two-thirds owed by small business), the ~$2bn Operation Protego GST fraud and a critical ANAO audit, and a multi-year performance-measure overhaul (several measures still 'under development'). The plan openly admits technology 'irritants', change-capacity constraints, cyber-threat exposure, and a strategic-commissioning shift toward reduced IT outsourcing. For the Consulting Firm, the strongest plays are program delivery and assurance of named reforms, fraud/payment-integrity analytics, performance-framework redesign, and workforce/operating-model change — calibrated to the firm's Commonwealth panels and the ATO's intent to insource core IT work.

Strategic priorities

  • Strengthen payment performance and debt collection — Reduce growth in collectable debt (over $50bn, much owed by small business) through the Payment Strategy: prevention, early intervention and firmer/faster actions, supported by data and analytics. Targets include reducing the collectable-debt-to-net-tax ratio to 7% then 6%.
  • Enhance counter-fraud measures — Deploy real-time monitoring and prevention controls, secure messaging and app enhancements to prevent, detect and respond to external fraud, following funding for the Counter Fraud Program.
  • Establish Payday Super — Implement the reform aligning employer Super Guarantee payments with wages (replacing quarterly payments), including administrative design, planning, and SG obligation management. A named, funded multi-year program.
  • Digitalised future tax experience for small business — Implement a streamlined, digitalised tax experience leveraging data-driven insights to reduce compliance burden, prevent debt and improve accuracy, moving toward a 'tax just happens' model.
  • Our Performance Evolution (Capability Review response) — Respond to the APSC Capability Review (published 27 March 2025) by sharpening strategic direction, aligning resourcing, strengthening risk-based decision-making, embedding collaboration, and addressing technology irritants.

Reform & change drivers

  • APSC Capability Review of the ATO listed entity (March 2025) driving 'Our Performance Evolution' enterprise change
  • Payday Super reform (2023-24 Budget) requiring new administrative design and SG system change from implementation
  • Counter Fraud Program funding following the ~$2bn Operation Protego GST refund fraud
  • Performance-measurement overhaul: purpose/key activities consolidated (6 to 4), multiple measures 'under development', and ongoing ANAO engagement to mature performance reporting
  • APS Strategic Commissioning Framework: targets to reduce outsourcing, especially in IT, in line with workforce plans
  • Retirement of Program 1.3 (ABRS) and transfer of business registers to ASIC; ABR Registrar responsibilities folded into Program 1.1
  • New tax-incentive administration to stand up: Hydrogen Production and Critical Minerals Production Tax Incentives from 1 July 2027
  • Self-assessing income tax exemption reforms driving a surge in ACNC charity registrations
  • TPB reform program (post-PwC tax-leaks): enhanced sanctions powers (from 1 July 2026) and registration framework changes (from 1 July 2027)
  • Tax Ombudsman recommendations on identification and management of financial abuse within the tax system
  • Rising cost-of-living, insolvencies and AI-enabled fraud reshaping taxpayer behaviour and risk

Challenges & risks

  • Payment and debt performance risk: debt rising to unacceptable levels driven by economic volatility or ineffective strategies
  • External fraud risk: agile, treatment-resilient fraud requiring a more modern, sustainable response than the current resource-intensive model
  • Change capacity and capability risk: insufficient capacity/capability to deliver on government and ATO priorities
  • Contemporary technology risk: ageing/strained technology environment, 'technology irritants', and demand pressures degrading security, reliability and usability
  • Cyber-threat risk: confidentiality/integrity/availability of ATO systems compromised by external actors or malicious insiders
  • Misuse of data and analytics risk, including AI governance gaps
  • Sustainable workforce risk amid digital, AI and integrity pressures and the strategic-commissioning insourcing shift
  • End-to-end service and case-management risk from complex internal operating arrangements and inconsistent decision-making
  • Standards and ethical conduct / integrity risk to reputation and public trust (heightened by staff-involvement findings in GST fraud)
  • ACNC capacity strain from an unpredictable surge in charity registrations driven by NFP self-review reforms

In the news & controversies

  • Operation Protego: the ATO uncovered large-scale GST refund fraud promoted on social media, with roughly $2bn in fraudulent liabilities (Apr 2022-Jun 2023), 57,000+ participants, and a further ~$2.7bn stopped before payment. (ATO (Operation Protego), https://www.ato.gov.au/about-ato/tax-avoidance/the-fight-against-tax-crime/our-focus/refund-fraud/gst-refund-fraud-attempts/operation-protego)
    Sustained, funded demand for fraud detection, identity/credential controls, payment-integrity analytics and assurance uplift — directly aligned to the Counter Fraud enterprise priority.
  • The ATO investigated around 150 of its own staff over suspected involvement in the GST fraud; some employees were terminated and ex-staff have been sentenced. (ACS Information Age, https://ia.acs.org.au/article/2024/ato-officials-terminated-over-tiktok-gst-fraud.html)
    Opportunity in insider-threat monitoring, internal-fraud controls, conduct/culture and integrity-framework work where independent advisers add credibility.
  • Draft retrospective foreign-resident CGT reform (flagged 2024-25 Budget) would broaden the regime and apply retrospectively, overriding two Federal Court rulings; described by the Tax Institute as 'highly controversial'. (Yahoo Finance Australia, https://au.finance.yahoo.com/news/ato-capital-gains-tax-overhaul-looms-for-foreign-investors-to-retroactively-pay-their-fair-share-031717488.html)
    Signals contested large-taxpayer administration and demand for policy-design, consultation and transition/implementation support.
  • Analysis of the ATO's 2024 Annual Report (first under Commissioner Rob Heferen) noted the ATO lost four of five Part IVA anti-avoidance cases at court in 2024 amid a firmer debt-recovery and litigation posture. (Corrs Chambers Westgarth, https://www.corrs.com.au/insights/ato-priorities-a-focus-on-large-and-multinational-taxpayers-in-2024-annual-report)
    Demand for legal-risk, dispute-strategy and case-management advisory in the large-corporate/multinational compliance program (non-legal advisory).

ANAO audit findings

  • An ANAO performance audit was critical of the ATO's safeguards following the ~$2bn GST fraud; coverage characterised the report as 'damning' the ATO's controls, while the ATO publicly defended its GST-fraud controls. (Accountants Daily, https://www.accountantsdaily.com.au/regulation/19602-audit-office-damns-ato-safeguards-in-wake-of-2bn-gst-fraud ; ATO statement, https://www.ato.gov.au/media-centre/statement-on-anao-gst-performance-audit)
    An audit-driven remediation backlog (recommendations to implement) is a classic entry point for control-uplift and independent assurance engagements.
  • ANAO's portfolio overview notes a prior-year qualified audit conclusion with unresolved findings on the ATO's annual performance statements, and ongoing moderate audit risk tied to a complex operating/IT environment and performance-reporting framework; the ATO featured in 15 performance audits between 2020-21 and 2024-25. (ANAO, https://www.anao.gov.au/work-program/portfolio/australian-taxation-office)
    Performance-measurement framework redesign, data assurance and reporting-systems work are live, recurring needs — reinforced by the many measures still 'under development' in the plan.
