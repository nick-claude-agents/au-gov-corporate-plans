Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet (PM&C)
Prime Minister and Cabinet · Corporate Plan 2024-25 · BD confidence: Medium

PM&C is the Commonwealth's central policy and coordination department, advising the Prime Minister and Cabinet and stewarding the APS. Its 2024-25 plan is deliberately stewardship- and people-focused: tight resources, growing policy complexity, and a strong integrity/culture agenda dominate, alongside coordination roles in Future Made in Australia, Net Zero, Working for Women, National Cabinet reforms and national security reviews. Critically for BD, PM&C has stood up Australian Government Consulting (AGC) in-house and is implementing the APS Strategic Commissioning Framework to REDUCE outsourcing of core strategy and organisational-transformation work — a clear headwind for traditional advisory pitches. The strongest openings are therefore in performance-framework assurance (ANAO is auditing PM&C's performance statements for the first time and flagged the absence of a complete enterprise performance framework), ICT/CabNet+ delivery, cyber resilience, and data work supporting Working for Women — not in core policy or org-design advisory.

Strategic priorities

  • People, culture and capability stewardship — The Secretary's overriding focus for the year is uplifting PM&C's own people, culture and capability to underpin its APS stewardship role, including major projects on workforce planning, organisational psychology and management capability under enterprise risk 4.
  • Integrity uplift (Louder Than Words) — PM&C is committed to actioning the APS Integrity Taskforce report 'Louder Than Words: An APS Integrity Action Plan', enhancing systems, processes and a pro-integrity culture at the centre of government.
  • Working for Women implementation — The Office for Women stewards the 10-year Working for Women gender equality strategy, with gender responsive budgeting, an annual Status of Women Report Card, a Women's Budget Statement and a year-one baseline data report.
  • Coordinating the net zero / Future Made in Australia agenda — PM&C coordinates whole-of-government delivery of the Net Zero Plan and six sectoral plans, the 2035 emissions target, and stands behind the new Net Zero Economy Authority and the Future Made in Australia agenda.
  • Reduce reliance on the external workforce — Under the APS Strategic Commissioning Framework, PM&C will reduce outsourcing of core work, targeting a reduction of up to $580,000 in 2024-25 in outsourced ICT/digital staff supporting CabNet+, and is expanding the in-house AGC capability.
  • ICT reliability and cyber resilience — PM&C delivers complex ICT to the PM, Cabinet and shared-service clients (including CabNet+) and is strengthening cybersecurity resilience against an evolving threat landscape.
  • Refreshed performance reporting framework — PM&C reduced from 17 to 11 performance measures and 5 to 3 key activities, introducing six new measures and committing to continue maturing its performance framework and reporting to Parliament.

Reform & change drivers

  • APS Strategic Commissioning Framework (Oct 2023) mandating reduced outsourcing of core work and published reduction targets
  • Stand-up and expansion of Australian Government Consulting (AGC) as an in-house APS consulting capability
  • APS Integrity Taskforce report 'Louder Than Words' driving integrity systems and culture reform
  • Working for Women: A Strategy for Gender Equality (10-year) and gender responsive budgeting
  • Establishment of the Net Zero Economy Authority and Future Made in Australia legislation
  • Independent Intelligence Review 2024 response and National Defence / AUKUS coordination
  • Set the Standard (Jenkins) parliamentary workplace reforms and establishment of the Independent Parliamentary Standards Commission
  • First-year inclusion of PM&C in the ANAO annual performance statements audit program
  • Budget and resourcing pressure against growing policy scope and complexity

Challenges & risks

  • Explicit budget pressure and tight resources against growing scope and policy complexity
  • Enterprise risk 4: lack of capability or capacity to deliver and meet emerging priorities
  • Enterprise risk 5 & 6: fit-for-purpose IT systems/services and protection of information, personnel and assets (cyber)
  • Absence of a complete enterprise-wide performance framework (flagged by ANAO) as PM&C enters performance-statements audit
  • Heavy reliance on stakeholder-survey proxy measures and 'timeliness' proxies for efficiency, indicating an immature performance-measurement approach
  • Strategic Commissioning Framework and AGC reduce appetite for external advisory on core work — a structural BD constraint
  • Integrity/culture exposure at the centre of government (whole-of-APS Robodebt aftermath, NACC environment)

In the news & controversies

  • PM&C coordinated and published the whole-of-government response to the Robodebt Royal Commission, and an APS-wide Centralised Code of Conduct Inquiry into public servants' Robodebt conduct ran into late 2024 — an integrity/culture matter within PM&C's APS-stewardship remit rather than a discrete PM&C failure. (PM&C — Government response to the Royal Commission into the Robodebt Scheme (https://www.pmc.gov.au/resources/government-response-royal-commission-robodebt-scheme); APSC statement on the Robodebt Centralised Code of Conduct Inquiry (https://www.apsc.gov.au/about-us/working-commission/who-we-are/media-releases-and-statements))
    APS integrity, ethics and reform-implementation/assurance work remains live and politically sensitive; capability framed around accountability, governance and culture uplift aligns to current demand, though PM&C will favour in-house delivery for core work.

ANAO audit findings

  • ANAO is auditing PM&C's 2024-25 annual performance statements (with NIAA) at the Finance Minister's request; 2024-25 is PM&C's first year in the performance-statements audit program, the risk rating is 'moderate', and a flagged key risk is the absence of a complete enterprise-wide performance framework through which PM&C plans, monitors and reports performance. (ANAO — Prime Minister and Cabinet portfolio work program (https://www.anao.gov.au/work-program/portfolio/prime-minister-and-cabinet))
    Concrete, time-bound demand for performance-framework design, KPI/key-activity mapping and audit-readiness support — the clearest single entry point in this plan.
  • ANAO lists a performance audit 'Administration of the Impact Analysis Framework' relevant to PM&C's Office of Impact Analysis; the audit listing was confirmed but specific findings/recommendations were not retrieved in the research. (ANAO — Administration of the Impact Analysis Framework (https://www.anao.gov.au/work/performance-audit/administration-of-the-impact-analysis-framework))
    Potential demand around impact-analysis/regulatory-policy process improvement and compliance uplift; confirm the audit's actual recommendations before pitching.

Parliamentary matters

  • The PM&C portfolio is scrutinised by the Senate Finance and Public Administration Legislation Committee at Estimates, with dedicated pages for 2024-25 Additional and Budget Estimates covering cross-portfolio (including Indigenous) matters; the research confirmed the pages exist but did not extract specific exchanges or questions on notice. (Parliament of Australia — PM&C portfolio, 2024-25 Additional Estimates (https://www.aph.gov.au/Parliamentary_Business/Senate_estimates/fpa/2024-25_Additional_estimates/pmc); 2024-25 Budget Estimates, FPA Committee (https://www.aph.gov.au/Parliamentary_Business/Senate_estimates/fpa/2024-25_Budget_estimates))
    Estimates transcripts and QoN are the best open-source signal of ministerial pressure points and bid-relevant priorities; a targeted follow-up pass would sharpen intelligence.
