Infrastructure, Transport, Regional Development, Communications, Sport & Arts · Corporate Plan 2024-25 (covering 2024-25 to 2027-28) · BD confidence: Medium

DITRDCA is a very large, multi-mandate policy, program and regulatory department spanning infrastructure (a $96.5bn 10-year investment pipeline), transport, regional development, communications, territories and the arts. Its 2024-25 plan is dominated by a self-improvement agenda driven by the 2023 APSC Capability Review, which found the department insular and weak in strategic policy, program management, data/digital and collaboration, plus a second year of ANAO Annual Performance Statements auditing it is racing to mature by 2025. Tangible enabling investments are named: a new Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) system, a 10-year technology/ICT modernisation plan with zero-trust security, a new Data Strategy 2025-29 and Data Governance Framework, and a Strategic Workforce Plan. On the program side, standing up the New Vehicle Efficiency Standard regulator (with new IT) and reforming infrastructure funding (the new Federation Funding Agreement Schedule) are time-bound. The key commercial caveat: the department explicitly commits to reducing reliance on consultants and external workforce, capping contractors at 8% of ASL under the APS Strategic Commissioning Framework, so advisory pitches must be framed around genuine specialist gaps and build/implementation work.

Strategic priorities

  • Deliver the APSC Capability Review response — Implement the Agency Action Plan through a Transformation Program to lift strategic policy, program management, data/digital literacy, collaboration and integrity, overseen by a Transformation Program Steering Committee, and track it via a new Capability Priorities Program Monitoring and Evaluation Framework.
  • Mature performance reporting for ANAO audit — Second year of ANAO Annual Performance Statements auditing; the department had no governance framework to assure performance information before the 2022-23 audit and is implementing a new Performance Framework to reach full maturity by 2025.
  • Stand up the New Vehicle Efficiency Standard regulator — Establish a new regulator to oversee the NVES (commencing 1 January 2025) including the necessary information technology investment, then move from development to implementation of regulatory functions.
  • Modernise ICT, data and cyber capability — Deliver a 10-year technology investment plan and future-state ICT architecture (zero-trust security), a new Data Strategy 2025-29 and Data Governance Framework, and explore/implement a new ERP system in line with the IT Roadmap.
  • Deliver major infrastructure and funding reform — Oversee the 10-year, $96.5bn Infrastructure Investment Program and finalise a new Federation Funding Agreement Schedule embedding reforms from the independent IIP and Infrastructure Australia reviews; continue Western Sydney Airport and Brisbane 2032 venue delivery.
  • Implement Revive (National Cultural Policy) — Deliver the 54 Office for the Arts-led Revive actions, expand Creative Australia (Music Australia, Creative Workplaces, First Nations Arts, Writing Australia) and strengthen First Nations languages funding.
  • Strategic workforce and integrity uplift — Implement the Strategic Workforce Plan 2024-2027 (APS Job Family Framework, capability frameworks), refresh the Integrity Strategy (reviewed 2024-25) and embed NACC/fraud-and-corruption obligations.

Reform & change drivers

  • 2023 APSC Capability Review findings requiring an enterprise-wide transformation and capability uplift
  • Second year of ANAO Annual Performance Statements audit and a commitment to full performance-framework maturity by 2025
  • New Vehicle Efficiency Standard Act 2024 and commencement of the NVES on 1 January 2025 requiring a new regulator and IT
  • Transport and Infrastructure Net Zero Roadmap and economy-wide net zero targets (43% by 2030, net zero by 2050)
  • Independent reviews of the Infrastructure Investment Program, the Land Transport NPA and Infrastructure Australia driving the new Federation Funding Agreement Schedule
  • New Commonwealth Fraud and Corruption Control Framework (1 July 2024) and the National Anti-Corruption Commission
  • APS Strategic Commissioning Framework driving reduced reliance on consultants/contractors (8% ASL cap)
  • Data and Digital Government Strategy alignment and rising AI adoption and cyber threat
  • Legislative reform agenda: Online Safety Act review, media/misinformation reforms, Creative Australia Act amendments
  • Commonwealth Climate Risk Disclosure requirements being built into risk management

Challenges & risks

  • Capability Review identified weaknesses in strategic policy, program management, data and digital, plus a perception the department is insular and reluctant to take external advice
  • Data capability described as 'largely devolved and inconsistent, with pockets of strength' and data handling a persistent APS skills shortage
  • Performance measurement immaturity flagged by the ANAO; many 2024-25 measures are brand new and unproven
  • Budget forecasting and business planning identified as needing improvement; ageing finance systems prompting ERP exploration
  • Tight labour markets and skills shortages in construction, transport and creative sectors
  • Pressure to cut external workforce/consultants to 8% of ASL while delivering an ambitious reform agenda
  • Construction-sector cost escalation and delivery risk across the IIP, Western Sydney Airport and Brisbane 2032
  • Cyber-attacks, evolving data governance and reliable service delivery named as operating-environment challenges
  • Complex multi-jurisdictional delivery dependence on states, territories and local government
