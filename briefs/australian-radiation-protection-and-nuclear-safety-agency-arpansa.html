Australian Radiation Protection and Nuclear Safety Agency (ARPANSA)
Health · Corporate Plan 2025-26 (covering 2025-26 to 2028-29) · BD confidence: Medium

ARPANSA is a small (~178 ASL), expertise-heavy Commonwealth regulator in the Health portfolio undergoing a period of unusual change driven by AUKUS, a growing nuclear footprint and digital modernisation. The dominant near-term event is the transfer of nuclear-powered submarine regulatory responsibility to the new Australian Naval Nuclear Power Safety Regulator (ANNPSR) from 1 November 2025, alongside an expanded national emergency-coordination role and a heavier ANSTO waste-facility licensing workload. The plan names several concrete, budgeted projects — replacing the ageing Cobalt-60 source and upgrading the Primary Standards Dosimetry Laboratory, the ANRDR/dose-register and customer-portal rebuilds, a laboratory information management system, a new website, and a cybersecurity-maturity uplift. The critical BD constraint is that ARPANSA explicitly states it has minimal consultant/contractor use and is committed to delivering work in-house under the APS Strategic Commissioning Framework, so opportunities are realistic only where specialist capacity or independent assurance is genuinely unavailable internally (notably digital build, cyber, costing and one-off project assurance).

Strategic priorities

  • Transfer of submarine regulation to ANNPSR — Facilitate the orderly transfer of regulatory control of Australia's nuclear-powered submarine enterprise and capability lifecycle to the new ANNPSR from 1 November 2025, including information-sharing, defining ongoing roles/interfaces, and continuing scientific and technical support.
  • Cobalt-60 source replacement and dosimetry lab upgrade — Purchase, install and commission a replacement Cobalt-60 teletherapy source (funded in the 2025-26 Budget) and upgrade the Primary Standards Dosimetry Laboratory to maintain Australia's sovereign calibration capability for radiotherapy.
  • Risk-informed regulatory uplift on RAD — Build on the new Regulatory Administration Database (RAD) to uplift inspection processes using a risk-informed graded approach, and expand regulatory-performance KPIs under RMG 128 (now 13 measures).
  • Radiological/nuclear emergency coordination (AUSRNEPLAN) — Develop the Australian Government Radiological and Nuclear Events Plan and fulfil the expanded role as Australian Government Coordinating Agency for radiological and nuclear incidents.
  • Radioactive waste management and national uniformity — Support licensing of three new ANSTO waste facilities (SyMo, ILWCI, NMMF), work with ARWA and state/territory regulators, and update the Radiation Protection Series to close regulatory gaps and promote national uniformity.
  • Workforce strategy refresh and digital modernisation — Revise the Workforce Strategy and develop a Knowledge and Learning Management Plan to counter knowledge loss and skills competition, while modernising digital foundations (cloud, cybersecurity, key systems).

Reform & change drivers

  • AUKUS optimal pathway and the standing-up of ANNPSR within Defence, increasing Australia's core radiation/nuclear regulators to 10
  • Australian Naval Nuclear Power Safety Act 2024 and draft Australian Naval Nuclear Power Safety Regulations requiring new frameworks and harmonisation
  • Decommissioning of end-of-life nuclear facilities and ARWA-led low/intermediate-level waste pathways, plus three new ANSTO facilities requiring licensing
  • ARPANSA's new designation as sector lead/Coordinating Agency for domestic nuclear/radiological emergency response
  • Whole-of-government digital and AI mandates (DTA guidance, PSPF, ISM) and a structured Microsoft Copilot adoption
  • 2025-26 Budget funding for the Cobalt-60 source replacement and capital infrastructure works across Miranda and Yallambie sites
  • Updates to the Radiation Protection Series following a completed national standards gap analysis
  • APS Strategic Commissioning Framework driving in-house delivery and tighter control of consultant/contractor use

Challenges & risks

  • Significant resourcing and operational pressure from acting as the sole submarine nuclear safety regulator until ANNPSR is operational, plus ongoing technical support thereafter
  • Increased workload absorbing ANSTO waste-facility licence assessments (SyMo, ILWCI, NMMF) on top of BAU
  • Failure to recruit, retain and support a highly specialised scientific/regulatory/corporate workforce amid competition from a growing nuclear sector and knowledge loss through retirements
  • Long-term financial sustainability risk to commercial service revenue and ability to meet mandated objectives
  • Cyber/security event risk and pressure to maintain PSPF and ISM compliance as a small agency
  • Regulatory capture risk and loss of confidence in ARPANSA as regulator
  • Compliance overhead disproportionately burdening a small agency (Privacy Act, PSPF updates)
  • Ageing scientific infrastructure and end-of-life assets across both sites
