Generated 30 May 2026 · Source corporate plan
Geoscience Australia
Industry, Science and Resources · Corporate Plan 2024-25 (covers 2024-25 to 2027-28) · BD confidence: Medium

Geoscience Australia is a mission-driven, data- and capital-intensive scientific agency (non-corporate Commonwealth entity) that has just secured significant long-term Budget funding for major new programs — most notably the 35-year Resourcing Australia's Prosperity (RAP) initiative, the Australia-US Landsat Next partnership, the design of the Critical Minerals Production Tax Incentive, and a new independent mineral testing function. These are foundational-year initiatives central to the Government's critical-minerals, net-zero and Future Made in Australia agendas, creating clear program-establishment, assurance and performance-framework demand. At the same time, the plan signals two important BD headwinds: a deliberate reduction in outsourcing of core work under the APS Strategic Commissioning Framework, and an admitted struggle to attract and retain scarce Earth-science, data-science and engineering talent. The best-fit opportunities are therefore specialist, independent advisory and assurance pieces (performance measures, probity, delivery assurance, governance/WHS implementation) rather than embedded core-science or core-corporate delivery.

Strategic priorities

  • Stand up Resourcing Australia's Prosperity (RAP) — Establish and begin delivery of the new 35-year RAP initiative that replaces and builds on Exploring for the Future, mapping critical and strategic minerals, groundwater and other resources to support the net-zero transition. The plan describes the next 12 months as laying the foundations.
  • Deliver the Landsat Next / Earth-observation partnership — Expand the multi-decadal partnership with the US Geological Survey and significantly enhance the Alice Springs ground station to be compatible with future Earth-observation satellites ahead of the 2031 launch.
  • Establish new critical-minerals functions — Support the design and development of the Critical Minerals Production Tax Incentive and establish a new independent mineral testing function.
  • Strengthen the performance and reporting framework — Continue a substantial overhaul of performance information — removing 16 measures, modifying 8, and adding new ones — and develop measures for new Budget initiatives (RAP, major assets under construction) for future reporting periods.
  • Reduce outsourcing of core work — Bring core capabilities in-house in line with the APS Strategic Commissioning Framework, targeting reductions in Accounting and Finance, Data and Research, ICT and Digital Solutions, Information and Knowledge Management, and Science.
  • Mature data, digital and ICT capability — Deliver the Data and Digital Strategy 2028 to make data and digital capability fit-for-purpose, secure and sustainable while managing rapidly growing data volumes and adopting AI, cloud and big-data tools.
  • Implement WHS review recommendations — Implement all recommendations from a commissioned review of the WHS management system covering physical and psychological risks, including sexual harassment and sexism.

Reform & change drivers

  • Major new long-term Budget funding for RAP (35 years), Landsat Next and critical-minerals functions, shifting the agency into a foundational establishment year for several large programs
  • Government critical-minerals, net-zero and Future Made in Australia agendas placing the agency at the centre of national resource policy
  • APS Reform agenda and Strategic Commissioning Framework requiring core capabilities to be brought in-house and outsourcing reduced
  • Refreshed National Science and Research Priorities and National Science Statement requiring re-alignment of science investment
  • Government Data and Digital Strategy and Public Data Policy driving data-management and digital uplift; emerging AI, cloud and quantum technologies
  • Commissioned WHS management-system review with a commitment to implement all recommendations, including psychosocial and sexual-harassment risks
  • Refresh of the risk management framework in 2024 to align with the Commonwealth Risk Management Policy and ISO 31000:2018
  • Large capital and infrastructure investment (Alice Springs ground station enhancement, Symonston building refresh, repository and monitoring networks)

Challenges & risks

  • Funding and resourcing may not support delivery of mandated functions (named strategic risk)
  • Inability to attract, develop and retain a skilled, diverse workforce in a highly competitive market for Earth-science, data-science and engineering skills (named strategic risk, reinforced by sector-wide 'geoscience crisis' reporting)
  • Risk of failing to appropriately acquire, preserve, protect, store and disseminate digital and physical information assets
  • Assets damaged or service delivery limited by natural disasters or extreme weather
  • Failure to maintain trust in programs, data, products and services
  • Avoidable WHS, environmental, community or cultural-heritage harm from field and other activities
  • Many new Budget programs (RAP, large assets under construction) lack mature performance measures, with 16 prior measures removed for lacking clear methodology — a performance-reporting gap
  • Reduced outsourcing appetite under the Strategic Commissioning Framework constrains the addressable market for delivery and core-function consulting
