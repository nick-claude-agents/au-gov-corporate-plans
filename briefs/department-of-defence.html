Despite the supplied URL/label referencing the Department of Defence, the document provided is Defence Housing Australia's Statement of Corporate Intent 2025-26. DHA is a corporate Commonwealth entity and Government Business Enterprise, self-funded through rents, fees, debt and land sales, that provides or supports ~80% of housing solutions for ~61,189 ADF members (forecast to grow to 80,000 by 2040). This year's agenda is deliberate and BD-relevant: accelerate end-to-end digitisation of customer and property-management services (including agentic/'industrial agent' AI), build an agile and scalable housing capability to meet ADF growth, establish an ESG baseline and strategy plus mandatory climate disclosures, and improve financial sustainability via an asset-management program and strategic procurement. DHA also flags a holistic KPI review in 2025-26. As a commercially-minded GBE under capital and cost pressure, the strongest fits are Digital & ICT Delivery, Financial & Accounting, Strategy/Policy/Governance and Data/Analytics, right-sized to a mid-scale entity.

Strategic priorities

  • Digitise quickly - end-to-end customer and property services — Reorient the operating model and leverage technology to deliver end-to-end digital services balancing customer satisfaction and commercial outcomes, with phased digitisation of tenancy/postings, enquiry management, rent allowance, online maintenance/inspections, then housing administration, LIA bookings, repairs/maintenance, tenant charges and vacancy management.
  • The case for growth - scalable housing capability — Shift strategic direction toward DHA playing a larger role in ADF housing, building future-ready, scalable provisioning responsive to market conditions and investor sentiment, including new sourcing channels and product models, and formalising future housing needs with Defence.
  • ESG baseline and strategy — Establish an ESG baseline defining how existing programs contribute to ESG objectives, then write an ESG Strategy with future activities and targets; meet EPBC Act and new mandatory Commonwealth climate disclosure / Scope 1-3 emissions reporting requirements.
  • Fiscal responsibility and long-term financial stability — Ensure DHA represents value for money via an Asset Management program to reduce repairs and maintenance costs (digitising asset accounting, focusing on preventive/programmed maintenance, an Asset Management Plan, digitised condition assessments and strategic procurement), targeting a 3% return on equity.
  • Holistic KPI review — Initiate a holistic review of KPIs in 2025-26 to be reflected in the 2026-27 PBS and Statement of Corporate Intent.

Reform & change drivers

  • Anticipated ADF growth from ~61,189 to 80,000 members by 2040, aligned to the National Defence Strategy 2024 and Defence Workforce Plan 2024-2034, driving a need to grow the housing portfolio
  • Constrained housing markets and tight rental supply, especially in priority regions (WA and northern Australia), reducing leasing flexibility
  • Access to capital pressure - DHA is self-funded and must achieve a sustainable owned/leased portfolio
  • New mandatory climate-related financial disclosure under the Commonwealth Climate Disclosure Policy (Finance-developed) from 2024-25, plus EPBC Act compliance
  • Technological change - agentic AI and 'industrial agent' off-the-shelf customer-service solutions as a digitisation enabler
  • Member and family wellbeing focus, with housing identified as a key wellbeing/retention factor (79.9% cite housing benefits as important to retention)
  • Negotiation of a Future Services Agreement and Housing Policy projects with Defence to formalise future housing needs

Challenges & risks

  • Self-funded GBE under cost pressure - must reduce repairs/maintenance costs while delivering a 3% return on equity
  • Securing leased properties in constrained, low-vacancy markets while needing to scale the portfolio
  • Building scalable provisioning and new sourcing channels in uncertain investor-sentiment conditions
  • Increased climate/disaster risk to the portfolio as more ADF members locate in northern Australia
  • Maturing ESG governance from a low base ('early in DHA's trajectory') while meeting new disclosure mandates
  • Delivering phased multi-year digitisation across customer and property-management functions without disrupting an 85% customer-service satisfaction level
  • Strategic-risk tolerance is a tracked KPI (target zero risks outside tolerance), implying active enterprise-risk pressure
