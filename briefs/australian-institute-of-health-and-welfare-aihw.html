Australian Institute of Health and Welfare (AIHW)
Health (Health, Disability and Ageing) · Corporate Plan 2025-26 (covering 2025-26 to 2028-29) · BD confidence: Medium

AIHW is a corporate Commonwealth entity and Australia's authoritative health and welfare data agency, now undertaking a significant strategy refresh built around 16 Strategic Initiatives delivered through a new Strategic Implementation Office (SIO). The dominant signals for the year are ICT modernisation (a 5-year roadmap and investment plan based on the AIHW Technology Strategy 2023-26), expansion of national data-linkage assets (National Health Data Hub, ANDII, National Disability Data Asset), heightened cybersecurity demands on sensitive data, a revamped PGPA performance framework, and a workforce/communications uplift. This is a data/scientific-agency archetype operating under explicit fiscal constraints, making enabling/corporate functions (ICT, data governance, project delivery, performance, cyber) the most accessible advisory entry points. The plan reveals real latent demand in program delivery assurance, data engineering, cyber assurance and performance-framework design. External research shows a low-controversy, low-audit-risk entity, so opportunities are capability-driven rather than crisis-driven.

Strategic priorities

  • ICT modernisation and a contemporary data ecosystem — Deliver the ICT Modernisation Roadmap and Investment Plan (under the AIHW Technology Strategy 2023-26), including an Enterprise Data and Analytics Ecosystem, enterprise workflow/business process management, enterprise information management, and elevated cyber and recovery responses to handle growing data volume, variety and security demands.
  • Expanding national data integration and linkage — Grow the National Health Data Hub (NHDH) and feed the Australian National Data Integration Infrastructure (ANDII) and its first asset, the National Disability Data Asset, plus new collections (primary care/GP, perinatal, mental health screening, FDSV, etc.) to meet rising demand for enduring, cross-jurisdictional linked data.
  • Delivering 16 Strategic Initiatives via the SIO — Stand up and run a dedicated Strategic Implementation Office to drive enhanced project management and program governance across the institute, with executive sponsorship and SIO oversight of deliverables, timelines, resources and risk.
  • Revamped performance framework — Implement a refreshed, strategy-aligned performance framework with new core-function, enabler and stakeholder-outcome measures to better measure progress, including many newly introduced metrics.
  • Cybersecurity and data protection uplift — Continuously elevate cyber and recovery responses and reach Essential 8 maturity goals to protect sensitive and increasingly demanded datasets in a rising global cyber-threat environment.
  • AI/ML capability build — Scale AI/ML adoption across business units, integrate AI into the enterprise data ecosystem, and update AI governance, risk and compliance frameworks in line with whole-of-government policy and the DTA's Responsible AI Guidelines.
  • Communications, stakeholder engagement and First Nations partnership — Diversify dissemination, develop internal/external communications and stakeholder engagement strategies, and pursue a new goal of being recognised by First Nations people and organisations as an exemplary partner, advancing Closing the Gap priority reforms.

Reform & change drivers

  • Department of Health, Disability and Ageing's expanded remit (disability services) and ambitious portfolio reform agenda AIHW is positioned to support
  • National Agreement on Closing the Gap Priority Reforms and Indigenous data governance/sovereignty commitments
  • Rising demand for integrated/linked data across sectors and jurisdictions (NHDH, ANDII, National Disability Data Asset, National Aged Care Data Asset, Child Wellbeing Data Asset)
  • National Primary Health Care Data Collection development (GP activity data; work with CSIRO on FHIR standards; National Primary and Acute Care Data Linkage Project)
  • Rapid technological change and AI/ML adoption under DTA Responsible AI Guidelines
  • Rising global cybersecurity threats against sensitive health and welfare data
  • Strategy refresh introducing new organisational goals, enabler priorities and a revised performance framework
  • Increased whole-of-government focus on monitoring, evaluation and measuring outcomes

Challenges & risks

  • Fiscal constraints explicitly named as a strategic risk for a corporate entity reliant on stakeholder funding
  • Cybersecurity and the reputational damage even a small data or privacy breach could cause to a trusted data agency
  • Increasing number, size and complexity of datasets straining ageing ICT systems
  • Complex legal, governance and privacy environment requiring high data-security standards
  • Resourcing and ICT funding strain on major data-linkage projects (NHDH, ANDII, National Disability Data Asset)
  • Workforce capability gaps — need for a formal workforce development plan and contemporary technical skills
  • Delivery risk across 16 concurrent strategic initiatives reliant on a small SIO and volunteer working groups
  • Largely new/unproven performance measures that will need validation
  • Academic critique that flagship reporting under-delivers on disaggregated/equity data (external)

In the news & controversies

  • A 2024 Critical Public Health article argued AIHW's burden-of-disease and suicide/self-harm monitoring work under-delivers on disaggregated data, aggregating groups broadly (e.g. CALD) and obscuring sub-group disparities. (Critical Public Health (Taylor & Francis) — https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/09581596.2024.2386333)
    A recurring, credible stakeholder demand for finer-grained, equity-focused analytics is a plausible advisory entry point in data design and intersectional analysis.
  • Peer-reviewed work notes AIHW's flagship Australia's health reporting has repeatedly acknowledged data and analysis gaps limiting monitoring of social determinants and health equity. (MJA / NCBI PMC — https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC9321841/)
    Self-acknowledged gaps in social-determinants and linked data signal demand for data-linkage and analytical-methods support.

ANAO audit findings

  • ANAO's Health and Aged Care portfolio overview lists AIHW as a corporate, non-material entity with a low risk of material misstatement. (ANAO Health and Aged Care portfolio — https://www.anao.gov.au/linky/health-and-aged-care-portfolio)
    Low audit-driven remediation demand; pursue capability/data-services work rather than audit-response engagements.
  • AIHW data is structurally embedded in audits of other Health entities — e.g. IHACPA receives hospital-level cost data and the NPHED from AIHW; AIHW data was cited in the COVID-19 audit activity report. (ANAO public hospital services audit — https://www.anao.gov.au/work/performance-audit/australian-government-funding-public-hospital-services-risk-management-data-monitoring-and-reporting)
    AIHW's datasets and standards expertise are valued across the whole Health portfolio, broadening where related advisory work could be positioned.
  • A historical 2008-09 ANAO protective-security audit concluded AIHW had not established sufficient practices for identifying and managing security risks (outside the recent window). (ANAO Security Risk Management, Auditor-General Report No.44 2008-09 — https://www.anao.gov.au/work/performance-audit/security-risk-management)
    Not a live opportunity, but indicates protective security/data governance has historically been a soft spot — relevant context for cyber and data-governance pitches.
