Australian Financial Security Authority (AFSA)
Treasury · Corporate Plan 2025-26 · BD confidence: Medium

AFSA is a small, expertise-heavy Treasury-portfolio regulator responsible for Australia's personal insolvency and personal property securities (PPSR) systems, plus criminal-asset management. Its 2025-26 agenda is dominated by a multi-year data and technology transformation (replacing legacy systems, building analytics/intelligence-led regulation and cyber resilience), workforce capability uplift, and a shift to a more visible, intelligence-led 'regulatory steward'. An ANAO performance audit into AFSA's management of conflicts of interest (Report No.4 2024-25) is a live governance/probity signal. Critically for BD, AFSA is explicitly cutting contractor/consultant use (over 40% in 2024-25, a further 1% target in 2025-26) under the APS Strategic Commissioning Framework, so opportunities must be specialist, time-bound and clearly non-core, with knowledge transfer built in.

Strategic priorities

  • Digital and data transformation — Replace outdated core systems with modern, scalable platforms and become a more effective data-driven regulator, delivering a Technology Strategy and building data/analytics capability.
  • Intelligence-led regulatory stewardship — Strengthen and target the regulatory posture using data, insights and intelligence, including an annual Regulatory Action Statement and a PPSR misuse detection pilot.
  • Workforce capability and culture — Deliver the Workforce Strategy 2023-28: build regulatory expertise, data literacy, leadership capability and a learning culture, while rebuilding employee engagement after a census dip.
  • Cyber security resilience — Continually improve protection of systems, data and people as digital delivery and cyber risks rise.
  • Vulnerability and access — Deliver targeted initiatives under the Vulnerability Strategy 2025-28 and improve clarity, reach and timeliness of information for under-represented groups.

Reform & change drivers

  • Partnership with Treasury on reform proposals to keep the personal insolvency and PPSR systems fair and balanced
  • Ministerial Statement of Expectations and Statement of Intent shaping key activities
  • APS Strategic Commissioning Framework driving reduced reliance on contractors/consultants and APS-as-default for core work
  • Economic pressure (rising unemployment, modest growth, elevated credit risk) lifting personal insolvency volumes
  • Emerging technology and digital assets (AI, cryptocurrency, NFTs) reshaping the credit system and creating new regulatory and intelligence demands
  • Legacy system replacement and an enterprise-wide data/technology uplift
  • ANAO performance audit into management of conflicts of interest

Challenges & risks

  • Funding sustainability named as an enterprise risk for a small agency with finite own-resourcing
  • Strategy and transformation risk - delivering the multi-year tech/data transformation
  • Employee engagement dipped (census index fell from 73 to 69 in 2024) requiring culture rebuild
  • Several performance measures are new or baseline-only (RAP1 baseline, ROE2 under development), signalling an immature outcomes-measurement framework for a regulator
  • Reliance on external platform managers (e.g. Fujitsu for PPSR) for critical register availability
  • Balancing regulating vulnerability against regulating misuse across competing stakeholder interests
  • Conflicts-of-interest governance under ANAO scrutiny

    System modernisation is the largest multi-year program in the plan, but AFSA is cutting consultants - the winning angle is specialist capability with built-in transfer to APS staff, not bums-on-seats.
  • High Prepare a short capability note on COI/governance framework uplift and independent assurance, timed to AFSA's response to ANAO Report No.4 2024-25, and seek a conversation with the Chief Risk Officer / audit and risk committee.
    An ANAO COI audit creates time-bound, defensible demand for governance and probity work that is corporate/enabling and less politically sensitive.
  • Medium Pull the ANAO audit's conclusions and recommendations verbatim before engaging, and watch aph.gov.au (Economics Legislation Committee Estimates) for AFSA appearances.
    The research confirmed the audit exists but not its findings; grounding the pitch in actual recommendations is essential and Estimates may reveal further pressure.

    AFSA's intelligence-led posture and active enforcement create demand for risk-targeting analytics, but the offer must map to its named harms to be credible.
  • Medium Qualify the consultant-reduction constraint early in any approach and frame all offerings as specialist, time-bound and transfer-focused.
    AFSA has cut contractors by 40%+ and defaults to APS for core work; ignoring this will sink otherwise-good proposals.

  • Funding sustainability is a named enterprise risk - an activity-based costing view of regulatory, registry and criminal-asset work can strengthen your funding case.
  • Several of your new measures are baseline-only - a PGPA-aligned redesign would give you defensible effectiveness measures for a regulator where public value is hard to show.
  • We understand you're reducing consultant reliance - everything we propose is specialist, time-bound and built around knowledge transfer.
