Department of Home Affairs
Home Affairs · Corporate Plan 2025-26 · BD confidence: High

Home Affairs is a large, complex policy-and-operations department spanning national security, cyber, critical infrastructure, immigration/citizenship, multicultural affairs and (through the ABF) border and customs. Its 2025-26 plan is dominated by post-MoG realignment (effective 17 July 2025), a multi-year Transformation Agenda responding to the 2024 APSC Capability Review, and a 10-year Digital Investment Plan to modernise visa, trade and travel systems. The department admits pressure on financial management capability, data governance maturity, workforce capability and regulatory maturity, while running named time-bound programs (Western Sydney Airport border facilities, Simplified Trade System, citizenship backlog reduction from 160,000 to 80,000, Nauru enduring capability, NZYQ/BVR community-protection arrangements). External signals — the Pezzullo sacking, Richardson contractor inquiry, and multiple ANAO findings on data governance and recommendation close-out — reinforce a department rebuilding governance, integrity and assurance. This is a high-value account with strong fits across Programs/Projects & Change, Digital & ICT Delivery, Audit & Assurance, Data/Analytics and Strategy/Governance, tempered by security-clearance and probity sensitivities.

Strategic priorities

  • Transformation Agenda and Capability Review response — Shift from foundational fixes to embedding long-term cultural and capability uplift across the department and ABF, delivering the Transformation Action Plan as the formal response to the 2024 APSC Capability Review across three pillars (Exemplary Leadership, Game Changing Agenda, Collaboration at our Core).
  • Digital and data modernisation — Implement the finalised 10-year Digital Investment Plan and Technology Investment Plan to modernise critical visa, trade and travel ICT and operational technology, uplift cyber resilience, establish data governance frameworks and adopt AI.
  • Visa and citizenship processing recovery — Reduce the on-hand citizenship caseload from 160,118 to 80,000 by 30 June 2026 through an uplift to over 300,000 decisions per annum, while maintaining migration program delivery and visa processing efficiency.
  • Border modernisation programs — Deliver design of standalone border facilities at Western Sydney International Airport (opening 2026), progress the Simplified Trade System microeconomic reform, and prepare for the 2032 Brisbane Olympics amid growing trade and travel volumes.
  • Financial sustainability and management capability — Lift financial management capability, budget discipline and management accountability, finalise and implement a 10-year capital investment plan and develop strategies for a sustainable fiscal outlook in response to Capability Review findings.
  • Regulatory maturity uplift — Invest in enterprise-wide regulatory capability and maturity across border, migration, critical infrastructure, cyber and transport security, aligning to RMG 128 with risk-based, data-driven regulation and improved performance monitoring and evaluation.
  • NZYQ/community protection and immigration compliance — Operate new community-protection arrangements for the NZYQ-BVR cohort (Community Protection Board, Operation AEGIS) and deliver character/cancellation considerations and detention-network assurance to manage community safety risk.
  • Integrity culture and anti-corruption — Build on the Integrity Culture Strategy, support the National Anti-Corruption Commission, and implement refined employee suitability and integrity procedures including an Insider Threat program.

Reform & change drivers

  • Machinery-of-Government changes announced 12 May 2025 and implemented 17 July 2025, realigning functions and expanding the portfolio (AFP, ASIO, AUSTRAC, ACIC, AIC, NEMA)
  • 2024 APSC Capability Review of Home Affairs driving the Transformation Action Plan
  • Data and Digital Government Strategy and the new 10-year Digital Investment Plan
  • 2023-2030 Australian Cyber Security Strategy (Horizon 1 completion end-2025, Horizon 2 implementation plan)
  • Security of Critical Infrastructure Act 2018 (SOCI) regulatory obligations and telecommunications security reforms
  • Safer Australia - Counter Terrorism and Violent Extremism Strategy 2025 and the new National Support and Intervention Program (NSIP)
  • NZYQ High Court decision driving Bridging (Removal Pending) visa and Community Protection Board arrangements
  • Migrant worker exploitation reforms and OMARA migration-agent regulation changes
  • Simplified Trade System microeconomic reform and Western Sydney Airport opening (2026); 2032 Brisbane Olympics
  • Strategic Commissioning Framework requiring conversion of labour-hire contractors to APS staff (40 FTE target in 2025-26 and 2026-27)
  • Citizenship caseload backlog (160,118) requiring uplift to 300,000+ decisions per annum

Challenges & risks

  • Workforce risk: attracting and retaining capability, psychosocial/WHS risk across diverse operational settings
  • Integrity and security risk: fraud, corruption, insider threat, cyber-enabled misconduct and protective security
  • Organisational compliance risk: lawful exercise of powers, currency of policies and processes
  • Planning and alignment risk: ensuring resources, systems and data are integrated and forward-looking
  • Data governance maturity gaps (flagged in plan and by ANAO) undermining data-driven decision-making
  • Financial sustainability pressure and need to lift financial management capability
  • Ageing/critical ICT underpinning visa, trade and travel that must remain resilient
  • Declining social cohesion and stakeholder/consultation fatigue
  • Contractor due diligence and procurement integrity weaknesses (per external inquiries)
  • Reputational and cultural rebuilding following leadership turmoil

In the news & controversies

  • Long-serving Secretary Mike Pezzullo was stood aside in September 2023 and dismissed on 27 November 2023 after an inquiry found at least 14 APS Code of Conduct breaches, including using his position for personal benefit and political commentary; he was later stripped of his Order of Australia honour. (Wikipedia, 'Mike Pezzullo' — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mike_Pezzullo)
    A leadership rupture at the top signals a department rebuilding governance, culture and integrity controls — demand for governance-uplift, probity and culture-change advisory, alongside heightened sensitivity around procurement propriety.
  • An inquiry by Dennis Richardson reportedly blamed senior public servants for years of failures that allowed billions in contracts to flow to companies linked to alleged serious crime, with warnings ignored for years. (Crikey, 14 Feb 2024 — https://www.crikey.com.au/2024/02/14/peter-dutton-home-affairs-scandal-kpmg-paladin-news-corp/)
    Indicates persistent weaknesses in contractor due diligence, vendor risk and contract assurance — an opening for procurement-controls, supplier-integrity vetting and contract governance advisory.
  • Home Affairs doubled the higher-education temporary graduate (subclass 485) visa fee from ~A$2,300 to ~A$4,600 with immediate effect, drawing criticism that it threatens Australia's foreign research-talent pipeline. (Nature, 20 Mar 2026 — https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-00822-3)
    Rapid, contested migration fee/policy changes create regulatory-impact, stakeholder-management and sector-engagement work and signal pressure to defend cost-recovery and integrity settings.

ANAO audit findings

  • ANAO Report No. 26 of 2023-24 concluded appropriate arrangements are not in place to support the regulation of migration agents, with missing/incomplete compliance strategy and plan documentation, and audit analysis hampered by poor departmental data quality. (ANAO — https://www.anao.gov.au/work/performance-audit/department-home-affairs-regulation-migration-agents)
    Clear demand for regulatory-operating-model design, compliance-framework development, data quality/governance remediation and SOP/process documentation in OMARA — directly relevant to Targets 47-48 in the plan.
  • The ANAO records six significant and three moderate unresolved findings from the 2023-24 performance statements audit, highlighting data governance/management maturity risks and revenue completeness/accuracy risks across a large distributed ICT environment. (ANAO, Home Affairs portfolio — https://www.anao.gov.au/work-program/portfolio/home-affairs)
    Strong, current pipeline for data governance, performance-measurement/assurance frameworks and ICT/revenue-controls uplift.
  • An ANAO audit on implementation of Parliamentary Committee and Auditor-General recommendations assessed at least one recommendation as 'not implemented' and noted the department contested a Category A finding; Management Action Plans were introduced from February 2022 to track recommendation owners. (ANAO — https://www.anao.gov.au/work/performance-audit/implementation-parliamentary-committee-and-auditor-general-recommendations-department-home-affairs)
    Indicates weaknesses in closing out audit/committee recommendations — an opening for remediation-tracking, assurance and Management Action Plan governance support.
  • ANAO Report No. 14 of 2022-23 found the media/communications branch's role and remit were not well defined, the structure created duplication and reduced effectiveness, client feedback was not processed within target timeframes, and an external call-centre provider underperformed in 2021-22. (ANAO — https://www.anao.gov.au/work/performance-audit/department-home-affairs-management-its-public-communications-and-media-activities)
    Scope for communications operating-model design, contact-centre performance management and KPI/contract-management improvement.
  • The ANAO lists a potential performance audit of 'Enterprise governance at the Department of Home Affairs', signalling continued audit focus on risk-oversight and internal-control systems. (ANAO — https://www.anao.gov.au/work/performance-audit/enterprise-governance-the-department-of-home-affairs)
    Pre-emptive governance and risk-framework uplift and assurance readiness work can position the department ahead of a likely audit.
