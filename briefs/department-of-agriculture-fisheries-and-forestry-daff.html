Department of Agriculture, Fisheries and Forestry (DAFF)
Agriculture, Fisheries and Forestry · Corporate Plan 2025-26 (covers 2025-26 to 2028-29) · BD confidence: High

DAFF is a large, regionally-distributed (6,900 staff, 159 offices) policy-and-regulatory department operating as a hybrid of policy department and national regulator (biosecurity, exports, levies). Its 2025-26 agenda is dominated by three strategic objectives — sector growth/trade, sector resilience and sustainability, and national biosecurity — underpinned by a multi-year Transformation Action Plan (TAP) and a 10-year digital investment roadmap that includes two flagship system modernisations (Simplified Targeting and Enhanced Processing System; Digital Services to Take Farmers to Markets) plus a 4-year ICT/security/asset refresh. Significant latent demand sits in digital/ICT delivery, regulatory maturity, program evaluation, data analytics, cost-recovery/financial management uplift, and emergency-management capability — several of which are reinforced by external pressure (an ANAO cost-recovery audit, an ANAO cultural-reform audit, industry calls for an independent audit of DAFF expenses, and a biosecurity levy controversy). Recent Secretary turnover adds strategic-direction uncertainty. The APS Strategic Commissioning Framework will constrain core-work outsourcing, so the firm should pitch specialised, time-bound advisory, assurance and build/implementation work rather than bodies-in-seats.

Strategic priorities

  • Modernising trade and biosecurity ICT systems — DAFF is delivering two flagship digital programs — the Simplified Targeting and Enhanced Processing System and Digital Services to Take Farmers to Markets — plus a 4-year security, technology and asset refresh, all under a 10-year digital investment and sustainment roadmap to address ICT technical debt and cyber risk.
  • Improving regulatory service delivery and maturity — A TAP priority for 2025-26 is to improve regulatory service delivery, lift enterprise regulatory maturity under RMG 128, and strengthen regulatory capability and culture across exports, biosecurity, imported food and levies.
  • Embedding an enterprise approach to program evaluation — DAFF is embedding an enterprise-wide program evaluation approach, with monitoring/evaluation/learning explicitly noted as 'underway' for major programs such as the Future Drought Fund and Climate-Smart Agriculture Program.
  • Strengthening data analytics and enterprise data maturity — Continuing to uplift data maturity through a new enterprise data strategy to support intelligence-led biosecurity, export operations and trade market access, and to improve corporate insight and performance.
  • Biosecurity 2030 and emergency-management preparedness — Implementing the DAFF Biosecurity 2030 Roadmap and National Biosecurity Strategy, becoming intelligence-led, and strengthening emergency-management capability (e.g. H5 bird flu preparedness) against a measured maturity baseline.
  • Enterprise financial management reform — A TAP priority 'in progress' to develop an enterprise-wide approach to financial management, uplift budget maturity via a forward-looking strategic roadmap, and manage cost-recovery arrangements under the Charging Framework.
  • Climate-smart agriculture and net-zero transition — Delivering the $302.1m Climate-Smart Agriculture Program, the Future Drought Fund, and the Agriculture and Land Sector Plan, with international engagement on emissions-reduction pathways.

Reform & change drivers

  • APS Reform agenda and the Transformation Action Plan (TAP) published Dec 2023, with 2025-26 focus on strategic policy, regulatory service delivery, evaluation and data maturity
  • APS Strategic Commissioning Framework requiring reduced outsourcing of core work in administration, accounting/finance, data/research, ICT/digital and program/project management
  • 10-year digital investment and sustainment roadmap plus 4-year security, technology and asset refresh, governed by a PMO with reporting to Finance and the DTA
  • Cost-recovery and charging pressure under the Australian Government Charging Framework (enterprise risk 5; consolidated levies measure SG-02)
  • Biosecurity 2030 Roadmap and National Biosecurity Strategy Action Plan 2024 driving intelligence-led, technology-enabled biosecurity
  • Climate risk: whole-of-government Climate Risk and Opportunity Management Program and annual Commonwealth climate disclosure
  • Geopolitical/trade volatility (new US tariffs, shifting global trade policy) affecting export market access
  • Emerging biosecurity threats (e.g. H5 highly pathogenic avian influenza) increasing emergency-management demands
  • Workforce attrition, ageing workforce and competitive labour market driving Workforce and Recruitment Strategies 2024-27

Challenges & risks

  • Nine enterprise risks named, including digital service-delivery capability, financial resources, regulatory obligations/outcomes, workforce capability, and security/critical infrastructure (cyber)
  • ICT technical debt and risk of system failures, explicitly driving the asset/security refresh
  • Cost-recovery sustainability and transparency, with industry distrust externally evident
  • Reliance on co-design and external delivery partners (states, RDCs, universities, NGOs) creating program-timing and expenditure risk for FDF and Climate-Smart programs
  • Weak/maturing performance measures: several measures newly introduced, qualitative, or flagged as 'to be reviewed', and a stated intent to develop outcome-oriented measures
  • Constraint on outsourcing core work under the Strategic Commissioning Framework, narrowing the kinds of engagements the firm can win
  • Leadership transition (Secretary departure reported externally) creating strategic-direction uncertainty
  • Cyber, data and personnel security risks under the Protective Security Policy Framework
