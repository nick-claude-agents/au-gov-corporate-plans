The supplied document is the Airservices Australia Corporate Plan 2025-26, not Services Australia (the metadata is wrong). Airservices is a corporate Commonwealth entity and Australia's air traffic management and aviation rescue fire fighting (ARFF) provider, operating with own-source 'airways' revenue under ACCC price oversight. The dominant theme this year is financial recovery: it is running an EBITDA loss in FY2024-25 (-$49.3m), heavily geared (87%), and has committed to the Minister to return to profitability and pay a dividend by FY2027-28, underpinned by a new long-term ACCC pricing agreement and an independent capital structure review. Simultaneously it is delivering large, multi-year transformation programs — OneSKY/CMATS, Western Sydney International, ARFF NexGen, the Enterprise Network Modernisation Program and digital aerodrome services — while uplifting culture (engagement 59%), workforce pipelines (ATC and firefighter trainees), cyber resilience and community-noise engagement. This combination of acute financial pressure plus a heavy capital/transformation portfolio makes it a strong prospect for financial-sustainability, costing, program-assurance, digital-delivery and workforce work.

Strategic priorities

  • Return towards profitability and sustainable pricing — Restore financial sustainability through service excellence, cost control, efficiency initiatives, new commercial revenue streams and a principled full-cost-recovery pricing framework agreed with the ACCC, targeting a return to profit and a dividend by FY2027-28.
  • Upgrade infrastructure and deliver new services — Modernise infrastructure and stand up new services — OneSKY/CMATS, Western Sydney International and Ballina, ARFF NexGen fleet/facilities, FIMS for uncrewed aircraft, and service-level upgrades — to meet a once-in-a-generation increase in aviation capacity.
  • Transform organisational culture and capability — Accelerate cultural transformation toward a safe, inclusive, high-performing workplace; lift engagement from 59% toward 65% (FY26) and 75%; improve role clarity, leadership and internal collaboration through targeted organisational transformation initiatives.
  • Improve service efficiency and effectiveness through technology — Invest in next-generation technologies and future-ready infrastructure (OneSKY, ENMP, digital aerodrome services, ARFF fleet renewal) with tighter fiscal control across operating and capital expenditure.
  • Build a future-ready workforce — Cultivate critical future skills — systems integration, data analytics, automation and cyber security — and recruit/train air traffic controllers and aviation fire fighters to address industry-wide workforce shortages.

Reform & change drivers

  • Aviation White Paper (2024) setting national priorities including airspace reform and the Aircraft Noise / Aviation Industry Ombudsman schemes
  • Ministerial Statement of Expectations requiring return to profitability and a dividend by FY2027-28 and independent biennial capital structure reviews
  • ACCC price-notification process and development of a long-term pricing agreement (FY2026-27 to FY2030-31) for full cost recovery
  • OneSKY/CMATS civil-military air traffic management harmonisation with Department of Defence (valued at ~$2.7bn over 20 years)
  • Opening of Western Sydney International Airport by end of 2026 and new services at Ballina; planning for Melbourne and Perth runways
  • Entry of uncrewed aircraft (UAS) and advanced air mobility, requiring FIMS and a UTM ecosystem
  • Net zero by 2050 commitment and Environmental Sustainability Strategy 2024-2030 (43% scope 1&2 reduction by 2030)
  • Full review and redesign of the Corporate Plan KPIs to strengthen legislative alignment
  • PFAS and asbestos contaminated-site remediation obligations exceeding current funding capacity

Challenges & risks

  • Financial sustainability: EBITDA loss, NPAT loss of $115.4m forecast FY25-26, gearing at 87%, and inability to set EBITDA / cost-per-IFR-hour targets beyond one year due to uncertainty
  • Revenue/pricing risk: heavy reliance on traffic volumes beyond Airservices' control and ACCC-regulated pricing
  • PFAS remediation costs acknowledged as beyond the current funding model, likely requiring Commonwealth and airport support
  • Delivery risk on major transformational projects (OneSKY, WSI) and the broader change/transformation portfolio
  • Workforce shortages in ATC and ARFF roles in a globally competitive, STEM/cyber-heavy talent market
  • Cultural challenges — engagement score of 59% with an ambition of 75%
  • Protective security and cyber threat exposure across critical operational technology
  • Climate change increasing frequency/severity of weather events that disrupt services and damage infrastructure
