Australian Commission on Safety and Quality in Health Care (ACSQHC)
Health (Health, Disability and Ageing) · Corporate Plan 2025-26 · BD confidence: Low

ACSQHC is a small, expertise-heavy statutory authority (regulator/standards-body archetype) that sets national health safety and quality standards, runs the NSQHS accreditation scheme, and publishes clinical guidance — funded on a cost-share basis by the Commonwealth and states/territories. Its 2025-26 plan signals several discrete, design-oriented pieces of work: a refreshed Strategic Plan 2025-30, an explicit review and redesign of its PGPA performance measures, development of a national quality measurement framework, an evaluation feeding the third edition of the NSQHS Standards, and a new Skill and Capability Framework. The catch for BD is real: the Commission is actively reducing outsourcing of core work under the APS Strategic Commissioning Framework (a ~$920,538 reduction target for 2025-26) and relies on the Department for shared services (finance, IT, payroll). Opportunities therefore exist but are modest, must be framed as specialist/one-off capability that genuinely cannot be insourced, and should be right-sized to a small entity. No ANAO findings, controversies or contentious parliamentary scrutiny surfaced — this is a stable, low-controversy body, so positioning should be capability-and-delivery, not crisis remediation.

Strategic priorities

  • High-quality care in an evolving environment — Responding to system change — AI, new models of care, virtual care, climate — by harmonising standards and guidance across sectors, including work on integrated systems and transitions of care, primary care, and appropriate use of digitally enabled care.
  • Strong outcome-focused clinical governance — Developing a national model for clinical governance and practical guidance for boards and health service organisations, and maintaining the NSQHS Standards (moving toward a third edition) and clinical care standards.
  • Empowering patients, carers and communities — Embedding patient, carer, consumer and community voice in healthcare design and clinical governance, including culturally safe care and First Nations health leadership.
  • An improvement-driven workforce culture — Building a quality measurement framework with clinical, patient-experience and outcomes measures, supporting reduction of unwarranted healthcare variation, and sustaining a patient safety culture.
  • Performance measure redesign — Reviewing and developing a new set of performance measures aligned to the Strategic Plan 2025-30 for future Portfolio Budget Statements and Corporate Plans, checked against PGPA Rule 16EA.

Reform & change drivers

  • New Strategic Plan 2025-30 and 2025-28 Work Plan driving a refresh of priorities and performance measures
  • Explicit review of PGPA performance measures against PGPA Rule 16EA
  • Development of a national quality measurement framework intended to become the architecture for future safety and quality reporting
  • Evaluation of NSQHS Standards impact commencing 2025-26 to inform the third edition
  • APS Strategic Commissioning Framework requiring reduction in outsourcing of core work (target reduction ~$920,538 for 2025-26)
  • Emergence of AI, virtual care and new models of care requiring new guidance and standards
  • Development of a new Skill and Capability Framework for staff
  • Innovate Reconciliation Action Plan 2025-27 implementation

Challenges & risks

  • Financial sustainability of the organisation named as a key risk (cost-share funded entity)
  • ICT systems risk — system failures, data availability, security breaches and misuse of AI
  • Procurement and contractual decisions affecting quality of outcomes and use of public monies
  • Project management risk in delivering work plan deliverables
  • Recruitment of appropriately skilled/experienced specialist staff to avoid work plan delays
  • Unmanaged expectation gap with stakeholders over deliverables
  • Quality of deliverables affecting reputation
  • Reduced ability to outsource core work under the Strategic Commissioning Framework, increasing reliance on internal capability
  • Few national safety and quality measures currently reported, complicating performance demonstration

Parliamentary matters
