Critical caveat up-front: the document supplied is the Department of Defence Corporate Plan 2025–29, NOT a corporate plan for the Defence and Veterans' Service Commission (DVSC). The DVSC is a separate, brand-new statutory oversight entity created on Recommendation 122 of the Royal Commission into Defence and Veteran Suicide; it commenced operations on 29 September 2025 and was re-based onto standalone legislation (Defence and Veterans' Service Commissioner Act) passed in March 2026. This brief therefore reads the DVSC's likely needs through (a) the external research and (b) the Defence plan's repeated references to implementing the Royal Commission recommendations under external oversight. The DVSC is a small, independence-driven body whose mandate is to oversee whole-of-ecosystem reform, advise government, engage families with lived experience, and run discrete inquiries (notably the $16.6m Inquiry into Military Sexual Violence in the ADF). The strongest, best-sized opportunities are establishment/transition support, governance and assurance build, performance-framework design, inquiry/evidence support and family-stakeholder engagement — all proportionate to a start-up statutory entity, not a large delivery program.

Strategic priorities

  • Independent oversight of Royal Commission reform implementation — DVSC's core mandate is to provide independent, arm's-length oversight of, and evidence-based advice on, reforms to suicide prevention and wellbeing across the Defence and veteran ecosystem, and to hold government to account on implementation of the Royal Commission's recommendations.
  • Standing up as a credible, genuinely independent statutory entity — Following stakeholder pressure for true arm's-length independence, the DVSC was transitioned from Part VIIIE of the Defence Act onto standalone legislation with a Governor-General-appointed Commissioner and explicit family functions. Bedding down powers, operating model and corporate functions is the immediate priority.
  • Delivering the Inquiry into Military Sexual Violence in the ADF — The DVSC has been funded $16.6m to deliver an independent Inquiry into Military Sexual Violence in the ADF — a discrete, high-profile, time-bound body of work requiring evidence-gathering, analysis and reporting capability.
  • Engaging families and lived-experience communities — A defining expectation of the DVSC is meaningful inclusion of bereaved families and lived-experience voices; stakeholder trust and transparency are explicit tests of its legitimacy.

Reform & change drivers

  • Recommendation 122 of the Royal Commission into Defence and Veteran Suicide directing creation of a permanent oversight body
  • Two-stage legislative establishment: enabling provisions (Feb 2025) then standalone Act (passed Mar 2026), requiring a fresh operating model and corporate set-up
  • $16.6m funded Inquiry into Military Sexual Violence in the ADF as an immediate workload
  • Senate Foreign Affairs, Defence and Trade Legislation Committee report (29 Aug 2025) shaping the entity's design and functions
  • Defence's own commitment that Royal Commission reforms will be 'subject to external oversight' (i.e. by the DVSC), and its performance target 4.4d to track Royal Commission recommendation implementation
  • Imminent ANAO performance-statements scrutiny across the Defence portfolio (Defence added to the ANAO annual performance statements audit program in 2024)

Challenges & risks

  • Start-up risk: a ~1-year-old entity with no settled corporate systems, governance frameworks, or audit track record
  • Independence credibility risk — every product is judged on whether it is genuinely arm's-length from Defence/DVA
  • Capability/scale risk: a small body must rapidly resource an inquiry and an oversight function in a competitive labour market
  • Reputational/sensitivity risk: working with bereaved families and survivors of military sexual violence demands trauma-informed, high-integrity practice
  • No external performance baseline yet — the entity must design measurable evidence of its own effectiveness ahead of ANAO scrutiny
  • APS Strategic Commissioning Framework and Defence's stated drive to reduce reliance on contractors/consultancy (in-housing of finance, ICT, procurement, project management) constrains the consulting market

    The committee report is the best public map of what stakeholders wanted fixed in the entity's design — use it to tailor governance and operating-model offers.

(Parliament of Australia, Bills Digest — https://www.aph.gov.au/Parliamentary_Business/Bills_Legislation/bd/bd2526/26bd044)

(Minister for Veterans' Affairs — https://minister.dva.gov.au/news-and-media/minister/defence-and-veterans-service-commissioner-bill-passes-parliament)
    Implementation of the new Act (standing up the Commissioner, new powers, family functions) is the immediate post-legislation work phase — typically high demand for transition and corporate-establishment support.
