Australian Criminal Intelligence Commission (ACIC)
Home Affairs · Corporate Plan 2025-26 (covering 2025-26 to 2028-29) · BD confidence: Medium

The ACIC is a Home Affairs portfolio statutory intelligence and information-sharing agency (security/intelligence/enforcement archetype) whose 2025-26 plan is dominated by the response to the 2024 Independent Review and the rollout of a new intelligence operating model. The agency is candid that it must replace legacy technology, uplift cyber posture, mature data governance, and stand up enterprise-wide data capability — all heavily ICT- and capability-driven demand. It is simultaneously bringing ICT and program/project management work in-house under the APS Strategic Commissioning Framework, which constrains some pure-staff-augmentation BD but leaves room for specialist design, assurance and build work. A history of high-profile ICT/program failure (Biometric Identification Services) and ongoing ANAO scrutiny (including a recent travel-compliance audit) create strong latent demand for independent program assurance, governance and internal-control uplift. The new performance framework (reduced from 11 to 6 measures) and a refreshed risk framework also signal evaluation and assurance opportunities, though clearance constraints and insourcing intent require realistic scoping.

Strategic priorities

  • Implement Independent Review recommendations and new operating model — The agency's stated major focus is implementing the recommendations of the 2024 Independent Review and standing up a new intelligence operating model to deliver more timely and relevant intelligence, contingent on available funding.
  • Modernise data, technology and ICT — Replace legacy technology, implement new solutions to operate at pace and scale, enhance cyber security posture, and mature enterprise-wide data capability including data governance, tooling and data fluency.
  • Evolve national policing information systems and NCIS — Continue developing the National Criminal Intelligence System — adding datasets, improving intelligence exploitation functionality — and sustain the 17 interlinked national policing information systems serving 71,000+ users.
  • Strengthen regulation of the National Police Checking Service — Operate as regulator of accredited bodies under a new access agreement (effective 1 January 2025) with stronger controls, and audit at least 10% of accredited bodies for compliance.
  • Workforce capability uplift and insourcing — Address critical skills gaps in data analytics, AI and cyber, develop a 5-year strategic people plan, and reduce outsourcing of ICT Digital Solutions and Portfolio/Program/Project Management under the APS Strategic Commissioning Framework.
  • Mature performance and risk frameworks — Embed the updated risk management framework and mature risk culture, and bed down a streamlined performance framework reduced from 11 to 6 measures aligned to Review recommendations.

Reform & change drivers

  • 2024 Independent Review of the ACIC and associated Commonwealth law enforcement arrangements driving operating-model and legislative reform
  • Pending parliamentary approval of comprehensive legislative reform to functions, powers and legislation
  • New intelligence operating model implementation, iteratively dependent on funding
  • APS Strategic Commissioning Framework requiring insourcing of ICT and program/project management
  • New National Police Checking Service accredited-body access agreement (effective 1 Jan 2025) strengthening the regulator role
  • Rapidly evolving, technology-enabled serious and organised crime threat (estimated $68bn/year cost to the economy)
  • Updated risk management framework (ISO 31000:2018, Commonwealth Risk Management Policy) requiring embedding
  • Streamlined performance framework aligned to Review recommendations

Challenges & risks

  • Technology and data enterprise risk: failure to implement, manage or secure data/services/systems/tools and stay ahead of emerging technology
  • Legacy technology requiring replacement and end-of-life system retirement
  • Security and integrity risk: protecting tradecraft, sensitive capabilities and against insider compromise
  • People and culture risk: national talent shortages and competition for data analytics, AI and cyber skills
  • Finance and resources risk: managing financial requirements, contracts, procurement and preventing fraud — with operating model implementation explicitly funding-dependent
  • Legal and ethical compliance risk with low risk appetite
  • Demonstrated history of major ICT/program failure (BIS) and ongoing ANAO scrutiny of governance and compliance

In the news & controversies

  • A 2024 independent review of the ACIC and associated Commonwealth law-enforcement arrangements called for the agency's remit to be narrowed, with commentary that more reform is still needed. (The Strategist (ASPI), 4 Dec 2024 — https://www.aspistrategist.org.au/review-calls-for-narrowing-australian-criminal-intelligence-commission-focus-but-more-needs-to-be-done/ ; APO, Nov 2024 — https://apo.org.au/node/329004 ; Dept of Home Affairs review page)
    A government-commissioned review signals an imminent operating-model/strategy reset — an entry point for organisational design, target-operating-model and reform-implementation work.
  • The ACIC's ~$50m+ Biometric Identification Services (BIS) program was scrapped after running late and over budget, with about $26m spent on the dumped NEC system (2018-19; outside the 3-year window but the agency's defining ICT controversy). (iTnews, 30 Nov 2018 — https://itnews.com.au/news/acic-spent-26m-on-dumped-biometrics-system-516291 ; Canberra Times, 24 Apr 2018; Computerworld)
    A high-profile major-ICT-procurement failure means strong residual demand and political sensitivity around program assurance, vendor management, business-case rigour and gateway reviews.
