Bureau of Meteorology (BoM)
Climate Change, Energy, the Environment and Water · Corporate Plan 2025-26 · BD confidence: Low

The Bureau is a mission-driven scientific/data agency operating capital- and technology-intensive observing and forecasting infrastructure under sustained funding and reputational pressure. The actual Corporate Plan 2025-26 text was not retrievable (the source returned only a landing page), so this brief is built from agency archetype and external research. The dominant, well-evidenced theme is the October-November 2025 website/ROBUST technology-program cost blowout (publicly stated $4.1m ballooning to ~$96.5m within an ~$800m program), which has triggered ministerial dissatisfaction, Senate Estimates scrutiny, leadership change and pointed criticism of consultant conduct. There is also a current ANAO performance audit on observing-network asset management, and persistent regional criticism of forecast accuracy. The clearest BD space is procurement/contract assurance, program governance, service/UX design remediation and stakeholder trust-rebuilding — but every approach must be conspicuously value-for-money and capability-transfer framed given live political sensitivity about consultants.

Strategic priorities

  • Technology modernisation (ROBUST) and digital service recovery — Stabilising and securing the large multi-tranche ROBUST technology program and recovering from the failed October 2025 website relaunch, including restoring radar and core functionality.
  • Observing-network infrastructure stewardship — Managing the physical assets underpinning the observing network — a focus reinforced by a current ANAO performance audit of asset management.
  • Rebuilding public and regional trust in forecasts and services — Addressing reputational damage from forecast-accuracy criticism and the website backlash from farmers, emergency services and meteorologists.

Reform & change drivers

  • Ministerial dissatisfaction (Minister Murray Watt requested a report on the website transition and cost) and a new CEO (Stuart Minchin) — a classic window for strategy resets and independent assurance.
  • Senate Estimates and parliamentary scrutiny of contract management and consultant conduct on the ROBUST/website program.
  • A current ANAO performance audit of observing-network asset management.
  • Funding and value-for-money pressure on an agency costing taxpayers >$300m annually while running an ~$800m technology program.

Challenges & risks

  • Severe contract-management and program-oversight failures admitted on a high-profile, politically sensitive technology program.
  • Public and regional trust deficit from forecast-accuracy criticism (Cyclone Jasper, SE Qld flooding, the El Nino outlook) and the botched website relaunch.
  • Acute political sensitivity to consultant spend — parliamentary criticism of 'big consultants' constrains how advisory work can be pitched and delivered.
  • Ageing/complex observing-network assets requiring lifecycle and assurance attention.

  • The BoM's new website cost rose roughly twentyfold from a stated $4.1m to ~$96.5m, revealed by new CEO Stuart Minchin as part of a wider ~$780.8m 'ROBUST' technology program funded across three tranches (2017-18 to 2020-21).   • The October 2025 site relaunch drew widespread backlash over navigation, radar lag, a changed storm-severity colour scale and removal of Doppler wind; the bureau reverted its radar map within a fortnight and apologised.
