Department of Social Services (DSS)
Social Services · Corporate Plan 2025-26 (covering FY2025-26 to 2028-29) · BD confidence: Medium

DSS is a policy and program-stewardship department (~3,000 staff, non-corporate Commonwealth entity) that designs social security and family payments and policy, with delivery executed largely through Services Australia and a large grants-funded community sector via the Community Grants Hub ($11.7bn, 32,034 grants in 2024-25). The clearest, most time-bound demand is the launch of Inclusive Employment Australia (IEA) on 1 November 2025 — replacing DES, run through an open tender and built on a new digital platform. Multiple multi-year reforms generate sustained latent demand: the National Plan to End Violence against Women and Children (with several named evaluations), Closing the Gap Priority Reform 3 (a Portfolio Transformation Plan), the Early Years Strategy, Paid Parental Leave expansion, and a backlog-pressured National Redress Scheme. New Data & Analytics and Evaluation Strategies (2025-27), DATA Scheme accreditation, admitted data/digital skills shortages, and heavy reliance on weakly-assured third-party performance data round out the picture. A critical BD constraint: DSS operates under the Strategic Commissioning Framework with a 90% APS-staffing target, so core policy work is kept in-house — specialist digital, data, program-assurance, evaluation and probity work is the realistic entry point.

Strategic priorities

  • Launch Inclusive Employment Australia (IEA) — Stand up a new specialist disability employment program from 1 November 2025 to replace DES, sourced via open tender and supported by a new digital platform and a refined performance framework, plus the new Centre for Inclusive Employment.
  • Steward the National Plan to End Violence against Women and Children 2022-2032 — Provide governance, coordination and delivery across 14 Commonwealth and 60+ jurisdictional agencies, run national initiatives (1800RESPECT, Our Watch, DV-alert, ANROWS, Stop it at the Start, KWSITH, Leaving Violence Payment) and report against six long-term outcomes.
  • Contribute to Closing the Gap Priority Reforms — Develop a Portfolio Transformation Plan for Priority Reform 3 and deliver Safe and Supported to reduce over-representation of First Nations children in out-of-home care, including Indigenous data governance.
  • Implement the Early Years Strategy and Paid Parental Leave expansion — Deliver action plans under the Early Years Strategy 2024-2034 and administer expanded Paid Parental Leave (24 weeks from July 2025, 26 from July 2026, plus 12% superannuation contributions).
  • Strengthen data, evidence and evaluation capability — Implement the new Data and Analytics Strategy 2025-27 and Evaluation Strategy 2025-27, move from interim to final DATA Scheme accreditation, and address admitted skills shortages in data and digital.
  • Process National Redress Scheme applications with quality and timeliness — Manage a large increase in applications while meeting new staged timeliness targets (25%/50%/75% within 6/9/12 months) and 95% decision quality, and maximise institution participation.

Reform & change drivers

  • IEA replacing DES from 1 November 2025 — open tender for providers plus a new digital platform build
  • Machinery of Government changes (revised AAOs, 13 May) transferring disability/NDIS, carers, social housing and homelessness functions to Health/Disability/Ageing and Treasury — a reshaped operating model
  • New Data and Analytics Strategy 2025-27 and Evaluation Strategy 2025-27; DATA Scheme accreditation transition under the Data Availability and Transparency Act 2022
  • National Plan to End Violence: multiple named independent evaluations (e.g. Our Watch PPAP over 2025-26/2026-27) and new outcome-based performance measures to develop
  • Closing the Gap Priority Reform 3 Portfolio Transformation Plan and Indigenous data governance commitments
  • Paid Parental Leave expansion (superannuation contributions, staged weeks increase)
  • Commonwealth Climate Disclosure requirements (governance, strategy, risk, metrics) and Net Zero in Government Operations by 2030
  • Strategic Commissioning Framework with a 90% APS-staffing target constraining outsourcing of core work
  • APS Reform Agenda, APS Integrity Taskforce recommendations and NACC obligations
  • Participation in the APS Capability Review Program

Challenges & risks

  • Heavy reliance on third-party delivery-partner data for performance reporting, with explicitly acknowledged 'minimal mechanisms' to assure some data — a clear assurance gap
  • Admitted skills shortages in data and digital capability
  • National Redress Scheme processing times lengthening amid a large increase in applications
  • Strategic risks named: Policy Development & Delivery, Integrity, Our People, Partnerships, and Systems and Data Integrity
  • Complex multi-jurisdictional coordination (14 Commonwealth + 60+ jurisdictional agencies under the National Plan) creating delivery and governance risk
  • Fraud and corruption exposure across payments, schemes and grants ($11.7bn through the Grants Hub)
  • Operating-model disruption following Machinery of Government transfers
  • Several newly-introduced or 'TBD' performance measures (e.g. National Plan outcomes, Our Watch, FWC 2.1.5-1B) signalling an immature measurement framework
