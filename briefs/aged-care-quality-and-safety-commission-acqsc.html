Aged Care Quality and Safety Commission (ACQSC) — Business Development Brief

Aged Care Quality and Safety Commission (ACQSC)
Health (Health, Disability and Ageing) · Corporate Plan 2025-26 · BD confidence: Medium

The ACQSC is a non-corporate Commonwealth regulator (regulator archetype) entering its most significant transition since establishment: the Aged Care Act 2024 commences 1 November 2025, introducing a new rights-based regulatory model, a new provider registration function, a statutory Complaints Commissioner, and re-baselined performance measures. The plan is dominated by implementation, capability uplift, new systems (the ACRES case management platform), a new Digital and Data Strategy, a new Enterprise Risk Management Framework, and governance/quality-assurance maturation. This is fertile ground for change delivery, assurance, governance, data and digital work. However, the plan explicitly commits to reducing outsourcing under the APS Strategic Commissioning Framework (a ~$4.5m cut targeted at digital and policy work) and to insourcing digital capability — a material headwind that pushes the credible Consulting Firm opportunities toward assurance, independent verification, costing, change and specialist surge support rather than core delivery.

Strategic priorities

  • Stand up the new Aged Care Act 2024 regulatory framework — The entire 2025-26 agenda centres on operationalising the new rights-based Act from 1 November 2025 — embedding a new regulatory model, transitioning the sector (including first-time-regulated providers), and realising reform benefits.
  • Launch the provider registration function — From November 2025 the Commission commences a new function of registering, renewing and revoking provider registrations — a new operational capability requiring processes, systems and decision-making at statutory timeframes.
  • Introduce the statutory Complaints Commissioner — A new statutory Complaints Commissioner is being established (subject to appointment), requiring a comprehensive engagement and communication strategy to introduce the role to the sector and empower older people.
  • Digital, data and case-management uplift — Embedding the new Aged Care Regulated Entity System (ACRES), delivering a new Digital and Data Strategy, enhancing case management and data platforms, cyber maturity and enterprise architecture — while insourcing capability.
  • Strengthen enterprise risk, governance and quality assurance — A new Enterprise Risk Management Framework, tiered risk governance, a Quality Assurance Framework, and integrated governance/privacy/'complaints about us' arrangements are being embedded to mature the Commission as a 'best practice regulator'.
  • Re-baseline and uplift the performance framework — Most performance measures are re-baselined to align with the Aged Care Act 2024, with several new baselines (provider/recipient confidence, non-compliance by cohort, home care assurance) to establish and evaluate.
  • Strengthen the Advisory Council per the 2023 Capability Review — Responding to the 2023 Independent Capability Review, the Advisory Council's independence, accountability and oversight role is being reformed and expanded under the new Act.

Reform & change drivers

  • Aged Care Act 2024 and Aged Care Rules commencing 1 November 2025 — a once-in-a-generation legislative overhaul superseding the 2018 Act
  • Royal Commission into Aged Care Quality and Safety recommendations continuing to drive reform
  • 2023 Independent Capability Review of the Commission (Advisory Council and broader capability uplift)
  • Statement of Expectations (28 May 2025) from the Minister and the Commission's Statement of Intent (11 June 2025)
  • New provider registration function and statutory Complaints Commissioner
  • APS Strategic Commissioning Framework — commitment to reduce outsourcing (~$4.5m, targeting digital solutions and policy work) and insource capability
  • Ageing population, rising demand for home and palliative care, and growth in dementia/complex care needs
  • Constrained aged care workforce supply, especially outside urban areas

Challenges & risks

  • Delivering regulatory outcomes within statutory timeframes from day one of a brand-new legislative regime (enterprise risk 4)
  • Attracting and retaining high-performing workforce capability amid rapid expansion (enterprise risk 1)
  • Governance arrangements and decision-making (delegations, timeliness, consistency, evidence base) not fit for purpose under the new Act (enterprise risk 6)
  • Information management, security and systems (including cyber, data integrity and usability) not fit for purpose, risking privacy/data breach (enterprise risk 8)
  • Effective financial resource management, including any changes to funding mechanisms (enterprise risk 9)
  • Business continuity / inability to manage disruption to critical functions (enterprise risk 10)
  • Stakeholder engagement and education not adequately disseminating key information during a high-change transition (enterprise risk 3)
  • Establishing credible baselines for new performance measures while a break in time-series occurs at Act commencement
  • Tension between needing transition support and the mandated reduction in outsourcing under the APS Strategic Commissioning Framework
