Inspector-General of Taxation and Taxation Ombudsman (IGTO)
Treasury · Corporate Plan 2023-24 to 2026-27 (Corporate Plan 2024-2027) · BD confidence: Medium

The IGTO is a micro statutory integrity agency (around 29 staff) that investigates taxpayer complaints/disputes about the ATO and Tax Practitioners Board and conducts systemic review investigations. The plan candidly admits acute resource pressure: a growing backlog of complex disputes, an 'unacceptably high' nine-month wait time, and forecast 5-10% annual growth in disputes as the ATO resumes compliance and debt-collection activity. Its single largest current initiative is implementing a new case management system (CMS) to digitally transform investigation services, alongside building an integrity framework to meet new NACC obligations and refreshing ATO protocols. As a Treasury-portfolio regulator/oversight body, demand fits data/analytics, digital delivery (CMS), governance/integrity, and workforce capability — but all opportunities must be right-sized to a tiny appropriation-funded agency that already outsources ICT and internal audit. A Senate inquiry into the IGTO's own performance adds a credible governance/capability signal.

Strategic priorities

  • Transform dispute investigation via a new case management system — Implement a modern, secure CMS giving complainants a real-time portal to track disputes, improving transparency on delays, caseload monitoring and reporting; later expanded to review investigations, stakeholder and information management.
  • Reduce backlog and improve timeliness of dispute investigations — Address an 'unacceptably high' nine-month wait time and a growing backlog of complex/highly complex disputes, including via the Feb 2023 joint IGTO-ATO triage/assessment process, while protecting staff wellbeing.
  • Foster fairness, integrity and accountability (NACC obligations) — Develop and implement an integrity framework and policies/processes to discharge obligations under the National Anti-Corruption Commission framework, as a named Commonwealth integrity agency from 1 July 2023.
  • Improve data systems and capability to capture and report evidence — Improve information and data capture, analysis and reporting to deliver meaningful reports and briefings that inform recommendations for improvement.
  • Improve community awareness of IGTO services — Raise awareness of the office, especially among vulnerable taxpayers and those outside metropolitan areas, via multi-channel communications and stakeholder leverage.
  • Refresh protocols and guidelines with the ATO — Refresh ATO protocols/guidelines to better communicate expectations, support effective investigations and enable escalation of significant matters to senior officers.
  • Attract and retain tax/superannuation specialists and build resilience — Establish IGTO as an employer of choice for scarce tax/super specialist and investigative talent, with training to AGIS standards and wellbeing support for staff in high-stress investigations.

Reform & change drivers

  • ATO resumption of compliance and debt-collection activities post-COVID, expected to drive a 5-10% annual rise in disputes (debt complaints were historically ~24% of receipts).
  • New NACC operations from 1 July 2023 making the IGTO a named Commonwealth integrity agency with new compliance obligations.
  • Economic pressures (high inflation, interest rates, debt, insolvency risk) increasing debt- and superannuation-related disputes.
  • ATO disclosure of tax debt to Credit Reporting Bureaus, which is paused where a dispute is lodged with IGTO - a direct workload driver.
  • Shift toward Complex and Highly Complex disputes that are resource- and time-intensive.
  • New CMS rollout (FY24) as a digital transformation of investigation services.
  • Planned abolition/replacement of the AAT affecting referral powers.

Challenges & risks

  • Nine-month dispute wait time and growing backlog of complex investigations against a 29-person workforce.
  • Micro-agency carrying the same mandatory corporate/compliance and reporting burden as the largest APS agencies.
  • Appropriation-dependent funding outside the agency's control, limiting capacity to scale.
  • Workforce risk: attracting and retaining scarce tax/super specialists; staff wellbeing in emotionally demanding roles (Key Risk 4).
  • Dependence on ATO for independent access to records/systems, with no sanction for non-compliance under the IGT Act.
  • Declining complainant survey results (59% service satisfaction in FY23 against a 70% target).
  • Reliance on external providers for ICT and internal audit.
  • Governance/effectiveness scrutiny via a Senate inquiry into IGTO performance.
