AgriFutures Australia (the trading name of RIRDC) is a corporate Commonwealth entity and rural Research and Development Corporation that invests statutory levy funds and matched Commonwealth funding on behalf of 13 levied industries plus emerging industries. It is a small, expertise- and grants-driven body whose core workload is commissioning, managing and extending R&D, and demonstrating value to levy payers and government. The most material BD signals are the new 2025-2034 Statutory Funding Agreement (incorporating 'levies modernisation') which triggers fresh performance, compliance and reporting obligations, and the cross-cutting national agendas it is leading - AI for agriculture (RDC AI Alliance), energy transition, connectivity and food/fuel supply-chain resilience. As a levy-funded entity it is sensitive to stakeholder legitimacy and probity, making governance, evaluation and probity work the strongest fits. Note: the supplied text is website content rather than the formal corporate plan, so several opportunities are inferred and confidence is calibrated accordingly.

Strategic priorities

  • Investing levy funds in industry R&D across 13 levied and emerging industries — Commissioning and managing research projects (e.g. honey bee Varroa control, chicken meat AMR, export fodder yield, thoroughbred fertility) and extending findings to levy-paying industries.
  • Leading national, cross-sector challenges — Driving coordinated national work on AI for agriculture (RDC AI Alliance), energy transition (Energy Transition Roadmap 2026-2035), on-farm connectivity and food/fuel supply-chain resilience.
  • Growing emerging industries — Building new industries (sesame, industrial hemp, insects) through frameworks, extension services and capability, with a dedicated Emerging Industries function.
  • Industry leadership and capability — Developing rural leaders and researchers through Horizon and Gary Sansom scholarships and industry advisory panels that direct levy investment.

Reform & change drivers

  • New 2025-2034 Statutory Funding Agreement took effect 1 January 2025, replacing the 2020-2030 agreement, to incorporate 'levies modernisation' legislative changes - creating fresh KPI, compliance and reporting obligations.
  • Introduction/administration of a compulsory Thoroughbred Breeders R&D levy following industry consultation, expanding levy governance responsibilities.
  • National policy momentum on AI in agriculture via the cross-RDC AI Alliance and a published situational/gap analysis.
  • Energy transition and on-farm connectivity agendas requiring new research and coordination.
  • Food and fuel supply-chain resilience pressures (National Food Supply Chain Assessment) drawing the entity into government coordination.

Challenges & risks

  • Reliance on levy revenue and matched Commonwealth funding makes financial sustainability sensitive to commodity cycles and levy-payer support.
  • Probity and conflict-of-interest exposure inherent in research-grant decision-making across many small industries (a documented ANAO focus area for RDCs).
  • Demonstrating measurable benefit/return on R&D investment to levy payers and government under a new funding agreement.
  • Stakeholder legitimacy in politically sensitive, welfare-adjacent sectors (e.g. thoroughbred breeding with a persistently low ~66% live-foal ratio).
  • Standing up new industries (sesame, hemp, insects) where commercial viability and evidence are still immature.
