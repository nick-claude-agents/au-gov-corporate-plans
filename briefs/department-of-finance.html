Department of Finance
Finance · Corporate Plan 2025-26 · BD confidence: Medium

The Department of Finance is a central agency and steward of the Commonwealth's resource-management, procurement, grants, risk and property frameworks, and operator of whole-of-government enabling services (GovCMS, GovTEAMS, ICON, shared services, GovAI). For 2025-26 it is focused on supporting the post-election fiscal agenda, accelerating safe AI adoption across the APS (GovAI), implementing the Data and Digital Government Strategy and Digital ID, building APS capability in the frameworks it owns, embedding APS Net Zero by 2030 and climate disclosure, and leading the National Security Office Precinct. Importantly for BD, Finance is deliberately insourcing ICT and reducing contractor spend by up to $17m to 2028-29 under the APS Strategic Commissioning Framework, so it explicitly favours external help only where independent or specialist skills cannot be sourced internally. The strongest, most credible openings are therefore specialist/independent assurance, evaluation, capability uplift, data governance and program-assurance work rather than commodity staff augmentation. As framework owner and the entity newly subject to ANAO performance-statement audit, it is a discerning buyer.

Strategic priorities

  • Accelerate safe AI adoption across the APS (GovAI) — Finance, with the DTA and APSC, is expanding GovAI from a 2024-25 pilot to APS-wide in 2025-26 as a foundational enabling service providing AI infrastructure, centralised applications and expert advice.
  • Implement the Data and Digital Government Strategy and Digital ID — Progressing initiatives in the Data and Digital Government Strategy Implementation Plan and broadening adoption of the economy-wide Digital ID System; ONDC operating the DATA Scheme.
  • Build APS capability in the frameworks Finance owns — Supporting capability uplift across the APS in AI, procurement, regulation, performance, accounting and finance through advice, training, communities of practice and outreach.
  • Strengthen procurement and grants integrity and transparency — As owner of the Commonwealth Procurement Framework and coordinated panels, enhancing procurement integrity, transparency and ethical supplier conduct.
  • Embed APS Net Zero by 2030 and climate disclosure — Leading APS emissions reporting and introducing climate disclosure requirements for Commonwealth entities and companies.
  • Deliver the National Security Office Precinct — Leading development of a permanent accommodation and capability solution for several national security and other Commonwealth agencies.
  • Progress regulatory reform and regtech for productivity — Driving modern, fit-for-purpose regulation in a digital age and improving regulator performance, capability and culture.

Reform & change drivers

  • Post-election government agenda: improving the structural budget position, lifting productivity, net zero transition
  • APS-wide AI adoption via GovAI and the need for safe, ethical AI infrastructure and advice
  • Data and Digital Government Strategy implementation and Digital ID System expansion
  • Commonwealth Climate Disclosure requirements and APS Net Zero by 2030 (emissions reporting now mandated)
  • Statutory review of the Data Availability and Transparency Act 2022 expected to report in 2025-26
  • Machinery-of-government transfer of parliamentary expense functions to IPEA from 1 July 2026
  • APS Strategic Commissioning Framework driving insourcing and a ~$17m reduction in contractor/ICT spend to 2028-29
  • First-ever ANAO audit of Finance's annual performance statements in 2024-25, raising the performance-measurement bar

Challenges & risks

  • Capability risk: sustaining the right skills internally while balancing limited use of external capability
  • Insourcing of ICT (incl. Central Budget Management System) reduces appetite for outsourced ICT delivery
  • Data risk: leading and applying effective governance, management, use and sharing of data across government
  • Digital disruption risk: preparing for AI and emerging technologies to disrupt strategic objectives
  • Reputation/agility risks tied to its central-agency advisory role and shifting government priorities
  • Climate risk to Commonwealth-owned assets under Comcover insurance and fiscal disaster-recovery pressures
  • Comcover financial sustainability (special account coverage and asset-to-liability ratio thresholds)
  • Delivery and compliance risk on the National Security Office Precinct (construction, security, accommodation)
