Department of Industry, Science and Resources (DISR)
Industry, Science and Resources · Corporate Plan 2025-26 to 2028-29 · BD confidence: High

DISR is a policy and program department central to the Government's 'Future Made in Australia' agenda, spanning industry growth, science and technology, and the resources sector. This year it is actively delivering a large set of named programs — National Battery Strategy/Battery Breakthrough Initiative, the National Reconstruction Fund integration, the Industry Growth Program, the Critical Minerals Strategy and Production Tax Incentive, the Critical Minerals Strategic Reserve, AI governance/National AI Centre, and major decommissioning/rehabilitation works (Northern Endeavour, Ranger, Rum Jungle, Maralinga). The department also runs significant shared-services delivery (Business Grants Hub for 11 agencies, payroll for 15+ agencies) and is uplifting integrity, risk, data/digital and AI capability. ANAO scrutiny is elevated: open performance-statement findings, in-progress audits of the NRF and AI/emerging-tech stewardship, and recurring decommissioning-provision valuation and grants-administration risks. The mix of named, time-bound programs under audit and review creates strong latent demand for program delivery, assurance, performance-framework, costing/valuation and data work.

Strategic priorities

  • Future Made in Australia industry delivery — Delivering a suite of named industry programs — Whyalla Steelworks transition, National Battery Strategy/Battery Breakthrough Initiative, green metals, Industry Growth Program, NRF Economic Resilience Program — alongside community benefit principles for FMIA recipients.
  • Critical minerals scale-up and Strategic Reserve — Delivering the Critical Minerals Strategy and Production Tax Incentive and standing up a Critical Minerals Strategic Reserve (via National Offtake Agreements and selective stockpiling) with a PM&C taskforce by the second half of 2026.
  • AI and critical-technology stewardship — Capturing AI opportunities while building trust and an appropriate regulatory system, via the National AI Centre, the National Framework for the Assurance of AI in Government, and growth in quantum and biotech.
  • Offshore decommissioning and mine rehabilitation — Delivering Northern Endeavour FPSO decommissioning (Phase 1 and procuring a Phase 2 lead contractor), Laminaria-Corallina remediation, Ranger and Rum Jungle rehabilitation, Maralinga maintenance and radioactive waste management via ARWA.
  • Data, digital and AI internal capability uplift — Implementing the integrated Data and Digital Strategy 2025-2027, deploying Copilot for Microsoft 365, building custom AI solutions, and maturing the Data Science and Data Management/Analytics Platforms.
  • Integrity and risk management uplift — Implementing APS Integrity Taskforce ('Louder than Words') recommendations, acting on the first Commonwealth Integrity Maturity Framework risk assessment, and developing a second Integrity Strategy 2025-27.

Reform & change drivers

  • Future Made in Australia agenda driving multiple new industry programs and funding vehicles
  • Net zero transition reshaping resources, energy and industrial decarbonisation policy
  • Critical Minerals Strategic Reserve to be established by H2 2026 with PM&C taskforce
  • Strategic examination of Australia's R&D system led by an independent expert panel
  • APS Strategic Commissioning Framework requiring progressive reduction in outsourced/contractor roles (ICT, administration, procurement in 2025-26)
  • Procurement reforms to leverage Commonwealth buying power and grow Australian industry participation
  • AI governance frameworks (National Framework for Assurance of AI in Government, AI Ethics Principles)
  • Commonwealth Integrity Maturity Framework and APS Integrity Taskforce recommendations
  • NOPSEMA reviewing its funding and cost-recovery model; reforms for financial assurance of offshore decommissioning

Challenges & risks

  • Delivery risk across a complex, expanding portfolio of named programs requiring adequate resourcing and oversight (named enterprise risk 'Delivery')
  • Maintaining confidence and trust of government, industry and public while delivering high-profile initiatives
  • Financial sustainability as a named operational risk, with open discussions to reallocate funding
  • Information and system security including AI and quantum cryptography threats
  • Integrity risk and need to embed a 'safe to speak up' culture
  • Workforce capability gaps in a new technology landscape, with reliance on external workforce only for highly specialised/independent roles
  • Valuation complexity of decommissioning/rehabilitation provisions (Northern Endeavour, Ranger) flagged by ANAO
  • Grants administration and large-investment oversight via the Business Grants Hub
  • Performance measures for overarching or early-stage initiatives are immature and evolving
