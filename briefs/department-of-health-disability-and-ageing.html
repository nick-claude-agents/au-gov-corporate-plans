It describes a hybrid policy-and-service-delivery department managing the largest reform agenda in its history: a $17.7bn aged care reform response to the Royal Commission (five pillars), a $2.3bn National Mental Health and Suicide Prevention Plan, the COVID-19 vaccine rollout (Operation COVID Shield), and major digital/data modernisation. Strategic priorities

  • Aged care generational reform — Implement the Government's $17.7bn, 5-year response to the Royal Commission into Aged Care Quality and Safety across five pillars (home care, residential services & sustainability, quality & safety, workforce, governance), including a new AN-ACC funding model from Oct 2022, a single assessment workforce from July 2023, star ratings, a new Aged Care Act, and a new prudential/financial monitoring framework.
  • Mental health and suicide prevention reform — Deliver the $2.3bn National Mental Health and Suicide Prevention Plan — a new National Agreement and bilateral agreements with states, new Head to Health and headspace centres, a National Suicide Prevention Office, and a National Mental Health Workforce Strategy.
  • Digital health and platform modernisation — Transform regulatory and subsidisation platforms (Health Products Portal), support the national digital health agenda and My Health Record, rationalise the aged care application landscape, and stand up a new Chief Technology Officer function to make technology platforms fit-for-purpose, financially sustainable and secure.
  • Data, evaluation and performance uplift — Deliver a consistent departmental data policy and governance framework, lift data literacy, link key datasets, embed a whole-of-department evaluation strategy, and complete a Performance Reporting Continuous Improvement Project to redesign PGPA performance measures.
  • Workforce strategy and New Ways of Working — Develop a new departmental workforce strategy, roll out the New Ways of Working program (workplace, technology and change), and build named health workforce strategies (National Medical Workforce Strategy, Nursing/NP plans, aged care workforce).
  • Financial sustainability and prudential oversight — Manage more than $115bn (19.5% of Commonwealth expenses) under a strengthened financial framework (Finance Strategy 2020–24), and build new prudential monitoring/compliance to identify and support aged care providers at financial risk.

Reform & change drivers

  • Royal Commission into Aged Care Quality and Safety — $17.7bn response with legislated timelines through 2025
  • Productivity Commission Inquiry into Mental Health and National Suicide Prevention Adviser reports driving the National Mental Health and Suicide Prevention Plan
  • New Australian National Aged Care Classification (AN-ACC) funding model from 1 October 2022
  • New Aged Care Act and new independent regulatory authority (governance pillar)
  • Single aged care assessment workforce replacing ACAT/RAS from July 2023; new single care-at-home program from July 2023
  • Alignment of regulation across aged care, NDIS/disability and veterans' care
  • COVID-19 vaccine rollout (Operation COVID Shield) and pandemic preparedness
  • Updated PGPA Rule s16EA performance-reporting requirements (March 2020) driving measure redesign
  • ICT Strategy and a new Chief Technology Officer role; Essential 8 cyber controls rollout

Challenges & risks

  • Named enterprise risks include insufficient data/IT capability and skilled staff to deliver health, ageing and sport services
  • Policy risk that health system strategy is insufficient to mitigate growth in outlays (cost/sustainability pressure on a $115bn+ portfolio)
  • Delivery risk: coordination across the department and external partners (states, providers) insufficient, with adverse budgetary effect
  • Aged care provider financial failure risk requiring prudential monitoring and market intervention
  • Failure to learn through measuring and evaluating policies, programs and service outcomes
  • Workforce attraction/retention and capability gaps across data, digital and HR
  • Newly introduced and immature performance measures (many baselines 'to be confirmed')
  • Stakeholder/delivery complexity of reforms requiring agreement with states and territories
